# Disliking / Hating Tendency — Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD)

**Discipline:** Psychology  ·  **Intensity:** lens  ·  **As-of:** 2026-06-01

## What this model predicts

Munger's *Disliking/Hating Tendency* says antipathy is an evolved, sticky force: once
a constituency comes to dislike a person or institution, it (1) ignores virtues and
exaggerates faults, (2) refuses to deal with them, and (3) distorts other facts to
keep the hatred consistent. Applied to an investment, the question is operational:
**who hates this company — regulators, customers, communities, ex-employees,
investors, the press — and can that hatred translate into concrete harm: regulation,
boycotts, talent flight, litigation, refusal-to-fund, or a permanently impaired
ability to do business?**

If hatred is *materially* present and *channelable* into harm, we should observe some
of:
- **X1 — Organized regulatory hostility** with a path to enforcement (not just generic
  rules everyone faces).
- **X2 — Customer/community antipathy** strong enough to suppress demand or trigger
  boycotts (a "refuse-to-deal" reaction).
- **X3 — Talent flight / inability to hire** the people the business needs, plus
  demoralized ex-employees broadcasting contempt.
- **X4 — Investor/counterparty antipathy** that raises the cost of capital, invites
  litigation, or makes funders demand punitive terms.
- **X5 — Reputational contempt in the press/peer community** that hardens into the
  "ignore virtues, exaggerate faults" pattern and is hard to reverse.

The model is *fatal-capable only loosely* (it is a psychology lens, not one of the
designated fatal models like incentives/inversion/accounting), so I will not lightly
call a fatal flaw — but I will flag where hatred compounds an already-fragile
situation.

**The mandatory self-check (Munger insists psychology models be turned on the
analyst).** Allbirds-as-NewBird is one of the most *mockable* companies in the market
right now — a wool-sneaker brand renaming itself an "AI" company. That makes it a
prime candidate for **our own** Disliking Tendency to fire: contempt is cheap, feels
righteous, and can masquerade as analysis. I must separate "this deserves ridicule"
from "this is a bad investment," and check whether the crowd's hatred is *information*
or merely *emotion*.

## Evidence

**Supporting — hatred/antipathy is real and unusually broad (cited):**

- **X5 — Press contempt is near-universal and derisive.** Mainstream and trade press
  uniformly framed the pivot as desperate and absurd: Slate ("the most bizarre pivot
  in tech," 2026-04); Fortune ("desperate AI pivot," 2026-04-15); Futurism
  ("Delusional AI Pivot"); The Register explicitly headlined it "following in the
  footsteps of Long Island Iced Tea," invoking a company that ended in **SEC delisting
  and insider-trading charges** (theregister.com, 2026-04-15). Yahoo Finance aggregated
  the reaction as "'I've seen this movie before.'" The dossier records IBKR's Sosnick,
  Sherwood, and Motley Fool calling it speculative, and Jim Cramer writing on X: "the
  first definitive sign that things have gone too far." (Dossier §7; web lens research.)
- **X3 — Ex-employee antipathy is vivid and explicitly aimed at management.** Glassdoor
  ~3.6/5 and **falling ~6% (one lane) / ~4% (Glassdoor page) YoY**, only ~51–56%
  recommend, ~13% positive outlook. Public review titles include *"Allbirds is going to
  go out of business and it's upper management's fault,"* *"it's chaos,"* and *"Great
  Products & Mission but Misguided and Poor Leadership."* Body text: "Corporate has no
  idea what they're doing and they're going to run this company into the ground,"
  "upper management is incompetent." Repeated layoffs (~9% corporate, May-2023; more in
  2024; **21 employees globally in May-2026**, per Q1-2026 filing). (Dossier §6; Glassdoor
  via web research; Retail Dive.)
- **X4 — Investor antipathy is structurally embedded.** Minority Class A holders were
  rendered powerless by a dual-class structure and a **~71% Support-Agreement lock-up**
  signed 2026-04-08 — outcome pre-decided over their objections. The company's **own
  DEFM14A** anticipates the lawsuit menu hatred produces: "securities class action
  litigation, stockholder derivative actions, fiduciary duty claims, books-and-records
  demands, appraisal-related claims… and regulatory inquiries in connection with the
  Asset Sale," plus claims over "alleged dilution, the change in corporate name and
  strategy, the elimination of public benefit corporation status, [and] the adequacy of
  disclosures." (Dossier §8; PREM14A/DEFM14A via web research.)
- **X4 — The one party still willing to fund it did so on punitive (hateful) terms.**
  The sole financier is a death-spiral convertible investor: 12% coupon, 5% OID, 17%
  late charge, 25% default/change-of-control premiums, sub-VWAP floating conversion,
  and a 55% co-investment lock on all future financings. These are the terms a funder
  imposes on a borrower nobody else will touch. (Dossier §2.4.)
- **X2 — A built-in stakeholder grievance: betrayal of the sustainability/PBC base.**
  Allbirds is a Delaware **public benefit corporation** whose entire brand was
  environmental. The Charter Amendment eliminates PBC status; the company's own proxy
  concedes "customers, employees, business partners, investors and other stakeholders
  may view that change negatively" and that it "may experience reputational harm,
  stakeholder criticism, litigation, derivative claims or other disputes relating to
  its change in business strategy, corporate identity, and public benefit orientation."
  (PREM14A via web research.) This is the textbook "refuse-to-deal" reaction from a
  base that bought the *mission*.
- **X1 (mild) — The pivot business inherits a hostile regulatory regime.** GPUaaS faces
  the **Remote Access Security Act** (House 369-22, Jan-2026; KYC/"cloud loophole"
  closure) and whipsawing China export controls (re-tightened 2026-05-31, covering
  Blackwell/Rubin). These are not *aimed at* NewBird, but they are real burdens a tiny,
  staff-light operator inherits. (Dossier §5.)

**Disconfirming — limits on how much hatred actually *harms* this specific situation
(cited):**

- **The thing most people "hate" — the legacy footwear business and its customers — is
  being sold and run off.** The forward entity (NewBird AI / a GPU-leasing shell) has
  **no customers to boycott** (market share ~0%, no disclosed customers besides one
  ~$2.75M QumulusAI lease). Customer/community antipathy (X2) therefore has **almost
  no operating surface to bite** going forward — you cannot boycott a company that
  doesn't sell to you. (Dossier §1, §4.)
- **Talent flight (X3) is partly moot.** The footwear org is being dismantled by
  design; ex-employee hatred of management, while loud, attaches to a business that is
  ending. For NewBird the issue is the *opposite* of flight — they have **no relevant
  AI/cloud talent to lose** and none has been named (Dossier §6). Hatred isn't driving
  talent away so much as the company never having had it.
- **Insiders are insulated, so investor hatred can't dislodge them.** The dual-class +
  lock-up means minority fury has **no mechanism** to change the outcome — which cuts
  both ways: hatred can't redirect the company, but it also can't (in the near term)
  derail the cash distribution that the *event-driven* thesis depends on.
- **No verified short-seller report and no confirmed filed class action as of
  2026-06-01.** The dossier notes "no short-seller report identified in 18 months" and
  the litigation is *anticipated risk-factor language*, not an active docket I could
  confirm. So the hatred is so far mostly *rhetorical and structural*, not yet
  *adjudicated*. (Dossier §8.)
- **A persistent ~16–18% short interest** is the financial expression of antipathy/
  skepticism — but into a 71%-locked "yes" vote it is *crowded and squeeze-prone both
  ways*, so it is as much a technical setup as a clean negative signal. (Dossier §7.)

## Reasoning

**Where the model is highly relevant.** Disliking/Hating Tendency is *more* relevant
here than to a typical operating company, because Allbirds has managed to alienate
nearly every constituency at once: a mission-driven customer base it is abandoning, an
employee base it has repeatedly cut and that openly blames leadership, minority
shareholders it has structurally disenfranchised and diluted, and a financial press
that has turned the company into a punchline and a fraud-adjacent analogy (Long
Blockchain). The model predicts each of these will "ignore the virtues, exaggerate the
faults, and refuse to deal." We observe exactly that pattern: there is essentially no
sympathetic constituency left.

**But the model's *operational bite* is blunted by the special-situation structure.**
The hatred's normal transmission channels are largely severed:
- Customer boycotts need a product to boycott — there isn't one going forward.
- Talent flight needs talent to flee — there's no AI bench to lose.
- Investor revolt needs votes — the dual-class lock-up removes them.

So the live channels through which hatred can *actually cause loss* narrow to two:
**(a) litigation/regulatory inquiry** (the company itself enumerates the docket it
fears — dilution, disclosure, PBC-elimination, fiduciary-duty claims), and **(b) cost
of capital / counterparty willingness**, where antipathy and pariah status have
*already* manifested as a death-spiral convertible and a distressed ABL lender. In a
shell whose entire common-equity value rests on a discretionary ~$1.34/sh distribution,
litigation that ties up or shrinks the wind-down estate, or financing that keeps
ratcheting against the equity, is the realistic way hatred converts to permanent loss.
Neither is a clean *fatal* mechanism by itself, but both **compound** an already
going-concern-qualified, negative-margin-of-safety situation. That is the model's real
contribution: it doesn't independently kill the thesis, it removes the soft tissue
(goodwill, benefit of the doubt, willing capital) that a fragile company would normally
rely on to survive a bad patch.

**The self-check (this is the load-bearing part for a psychology model).** I have to
take seriously that the *market's* critics — and I — are exhibiting Disliking Tendency
ourselves. The Long Blockchain meme, the "delusional"/"desperate" headlines, and the
reflexive mockery are *emotionally* satisfying and could be substituting for analysis.
The honest test: **is the contempt tracking the fundamentals, or contradicting them?**
Here it tracks them. Independent, non-emotional anchors — the company's own fairness
opinion ($0.02–$1.83/sh), the arms-length whole-company bid ($2.00–$2.25), the ~$1.34
estimated distribution, the pre-revenue GPU stub, the negative net cash — *all*
support the same conclusion the haters reach, by entirely different roads. So in this
case the bias and the facts point the same way, which means the **risk is over-
confidence, not wrong direction**: I should resist letting the ridicule make me *more*
certain than the (already damning) numbers justify, and I should be alert to the one
way contempt could mislead — a hated, heavily-shorted, low-float name is exactly the
setup for a violent *upward* squeeze that has nothing to do with value (Dossier §7).
Hating the company is not a reason to be short it. It is also not, by itself, the
reason to pass — the valuation is.

**Net read.** Antipathy is real, broad, and unusually total — a genuine *negative*
signal that confirms reputational and capital-access impairment and raises litigation
risk. But because the operating surface that hatred normally attacks is being sold off,
the model's verdict is **negative-but-not-fatal**: it adds conviction to a pass without
being the proximate cause of one, and it carries an explicit warning against our own
contempt hardening into either false certainty or a careless short.

## What would flip this read (disconfirmers)

- **A filed, surviving securities/derivative class action or a confirmed regulatory
  inquiry** post-2026-06-01 would *strengthen* the negative (channel (a) realized);
  conversely, a clean closing with no litigation and full ~$1.34/sh paid on schedule
  would show the hatred *failed to bite* and downgrade this model toward neutral.
- **Evidence the distribution is paid in full and on time** despite all the antipathy
  would confirm that, for an event-driven holder, the hatred is noise — flipping the
  *operational* relevance of this model toward low even if the *sentiment* stays
  negative.
- **A credible AI/cloud executive hire or a real anchor GPU customer** would blunt the
  "no talent / no demand to lose" disconfirmer and the press contempt simultaneously,
  weakening (not reversing) the negative.
- **A short-squeeze melt-up on the June-3 "yes"** would vividly demonstrate that
  acting on contempt (shorting the hated name) is dangerous — reinforcing the
  self-check, not the thesis.
- **A whistleblower/short-seller report alleging fraud** (the Long Blockchain endgame)
  would escalate this toward a fatal-adjacent flag.

**Bottom line:** Few public companies are this universally disliked — by the press
(Long Blockchain comparisons), ex-employees ("upper management's fault"), the
abandoned sustainability base (PBC status being stripped), and disenfranchised minority
holders — and the only capital still willing to fund it did so on death-spiral terms,
the financial signature of pariah status. But because the legacy product and its
customers are being sold off, hatred's normal weapons (boycotts, demand collapse,
talent flight, shareholder revolt) have little operating surface to bite; the live
channels are **litigation/regulatory inquiry** and **punitive cost of capital**, which
*compound* an already going-concern, negative-margin-of-safety setup without
independently killing it. Crucially, the contempt here *tracks* the fundamentals rather
than contradicting them — so the discipline's main warning is turned on us: do not let
righteous mockery harden into false certainty, and never treat "this is hateable" as a
reason to short a low-float, squeeze-prone name. Negative signal; confirms the PASS;
not, by itself, fatal.
