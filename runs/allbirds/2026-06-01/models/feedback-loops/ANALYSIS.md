# Feedback Loops ‚Äî Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD)

**Discipline:** Physics  ¬∑  **Intensity:** standard  ¬∑  **As-of:** 2026-06-01

## What this model predicts

A business is a system of loops. **Reinforcing (positive) loops** compound an
advantage: scale ‚Üí lower unit cost ‚Üí lower price (or more reinvestment) ‚Üí more
volume ‚Üí more scale (the classic Costco/Amazon "flywheel"). **Balancing
(negative) loops** restore an equilibrium or, when they turn malignant,
*decay*: lost volume ‚Üí lost scale ‚Üí higher unit cost ‚Üí higher price/worse value ‚Üí
more lost volume (a "doom loop"). The model asks three questions:

1. **Which loops exist** in this system, and which are reinforcing vs balancing?
2. **Which dominate now** ‚Äî i.e., where is the marginal energy going?
3. **Which way is each loop turning** ‚Äî virtuous (compounding up) or vicious
   (compounding down)?

If Allbirds were a Munger-grade compounder, I would expect to see a *virtuous*
reinforcing loop spinning up: rising volume lowering unit cost, funding either
price cuts that win more volume or reinvestment that widens the moat, with cash
generation feeding the next turn. Concretely, the observables would be: revenue
*growth*, *stable-or-rising* gross margin, *falling* CAC/rising repeat rate,
*positive and growing* free cash flow, and a *strengthening* balance sheet ‚Äî
each turn making the next turn easier.

The opposite pattern ‚Äî revenue contraction, margin compression, cash burn,
balance-sheet erosion, and financing terms that get *worse* as the stock falls ‚Äî
is the signature of **dominant vicious loops**. That is the test.

## Evidence

This is unusual: there are **three distinct loop systems** stacked on one ticker.
I treat each separately because they turn at different speeds and in different
directions, and the synthesis matters.

### System A ‚Äî Legacy footwear DTC (being divested): a textbook decay loop

**Disconfirming for a virtuous loop / confirming a vicious one** (cited):

- **Revenue compounding *down*, accelerating.** Net revenue peaked at $297.8M
  (FY2022) and fell to $152.5M (FY2025) ‚Äî ‚àí48.8% cumulative ‚Äî then **‚àí30.5% YoY
  in Q1-2026** vs ‚àí19.7% for FY2025. The rate of decline is *increasing*, the
  signature of a reinforcing loop turned vicious. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.1, ¬ß2.2; FY2025
  10-K MD&A.)
- **Gross margin compounding down.** From low-50s% ‚Üí 41.0% (FY2025) ‚Üí **27.8%
  (Q1-2026)** on "clearance/discount selling." (DOSSIER ¬ß2.1‚Äì2.2.) Discounting to
  move stagnant volume *destroys* margin, which removes cash to reinvest in the
  brand, which weakens demand, which forces more discounting. This is the
  canonical apparel "death spiral" ‚Äî independently described as: "scale spend ‚Üí
  CAC rises ‚Üí margin already thin ‚Üí cash runs out before LTV catches up"
  (Retail Dive / Top Growth Marketing, 2025‚Äì26). A near-identical mechanic
  played out at City Chic Collective, whose statutory gross margin collapsed
  48.8% (FY21) ‚Üí 28.1% (FY23) clearing an inventory stockpile (Eightx teardown,
  2026) ‚Äî the same number Allbirds just printed.
- **Scale unwinding physically.** Store count **60 (2024) ‚Üí 23 (late 2025)**.
  Fewer stores ‚Üí less brand presence and fixed-cost absorption ‚Üí lower volume ‚Üí
  more closures. (DOSSIER ¬ß1.) Capex cut to maintenance-only (~$3M, ~2% of
  revenue) ‚Äî the loop is being *starved*, not fed. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.2.)
- **Recurring inventory write-downs** every year 2022‚Äì2025 ($14.4M/$8.3M/$2.7M/
  $2.8M) ‚Äî chronic over-buy vs collapsing sell-through, the inventory arm of the
  decay loop. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.2.)
- **Cash arm of the loop.** Negative FCF every year; cumulative net loss
  FY2020‚Äì25 ‚âà ‚àí$495.6M; cash $66.7M ‚Üí $26.7M ‚Üí $14.4M (3/31/2026); **net cash
  flipped negative.** Burn forces dilution/distressed debt, which signals
  weakness, which depresses the equity, which raises the cost of the next dollar.
  (DOSSIER ¬ß2.2‚Äì2.3.)
- **Competitive arm.** Volume bled to On (+32‚Äì36% YoY) and Hoka (FY2025 $2.23B,
  +23.6%); the sustainability differentiator was commoditized by Nike/Adidas. A
  rival's virtuous loop is the mirror image of Allbirds' vicious one ‚Äî share is a
  conserved quantity here. (DOSSIER ¬ß4.)

**There is essentially no supporting evidence for a virtuous loop in the legacy
business.** The one mild offset ‚Äî gross margin briefly *rising* FY2023‚ÜíFY2024
(41.0%‚Üí42.7%) ‚Äî was a one-off from clearing low-margin inventory and pulling back
promotions, not a flywheel; it reversed hard in Q1-2026. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.1.)

[FACT-PATTERN JUDGMENT] The legacy loop is unambiguously a **dominant, accelerating
decay loop.** The market's verdict confirms the physics: ~$4.1B peak value ‚Üí
$39M IP sale ‚âà **99% destruction.** And critically ‚Äî **this loop is being
*terminated*, not reversed**: the asset sale stops the burn by selling the asset,
not by restarting the flywheel. (DOSSIER ¬ß1, ¬ß3, ¬ß6.)

### System B ‚Äî NewBird AI / GPUaaS (the go-forward bet): trying to enter someone else's flywheel from the wrong end

The whole GPUaaS industry is built on a brutally steep reinforcing loop, and the
energy is going almost entirely to the incumbents.

**Disconfirming for NewBird (confirming the flywheel belongs to others)** (cited):

- **CoreWeave's flywheel is spinning at maximum.** FY2025 rev $5.1B (+168%);
  revenue **backlog ~$100B**; **>1 GW active power, plan >8 GW by 2030**; NVIDIA
  invested **$2B** in CoreWeave equity (NVIDIA "Elite"); achieved an
  **investment-grade-rated** delayed-draw term loan; Meta committed ~$21B through
  2032; Jane Street ~$6B + $1B equity. (CoreWeave FY2026 8-Ks, SEC; DOSSIER ¬ß4.)
  Every element is a reinforcing-loop node: capital ‚Üí GPU allocation ‚Üí power ‚Üí
  anchor customers ‚Üí backlog ‚Üí cheaper capital ‚Üí more GPUs. The loop *feeds
  itself*.
- **The binding constraint of the loop is power, and NewBird has none.** Power
  allocation gates who can build; ~half of 2026-planned US AI datacenters are
  canceled/delayed; new interconnection entrants can't draw power until
  ~2030‚Äì2033. The cost-advantaged pivots that *do* win (TeraWulf, IREN, Applied
  Digital) start with **pre-existing gigawatt-scale power + capital** ‚Äî exactly
  what NewBird lacks. (DOSSIER ¬ß4, ¬ß5.)
- **NewBird's position on the loop.** A **single ~$2.75M, 3-year GPU lease to one
  QumulusAI subsidiary** is the *entire* operating asset; ~0% market share, no
  disclosed customers/utilization/backlog. It sits ~4 orders of magnitude below
  CoreWeave by GPU count and *below the rated universe* of 209 providers.
  (DOSSIER ¬ß4; 8-K acc 0001193125-26-164338.)
- **Sub-scale economics run the loop in reverse.** Post-depreciation gross
  margins 14‚Äì16%; capital recovery needed in 48‚Äì60 months *before* the next GPU
  generation; GPU rental rates ~‚àí50% over five years and H100 on-demand already
  ‚àí64% from peak. (DOSSIER ¬ß4, ¬ß5.) For a sub-scale entrant, falling prices ‚Üí
  thin/negative spread ‚Üí can't fund the next GPU vintage ‚Üí falls further behind
  on scale ‚Üí worse cost position. The flywheel that lifts CoreWeave **grinds
  NewBird down.** Vultr (2026-01-28): small-scale survival "unlikely."
  Constellation Research (2026-04-15): NewBird "is going to need a lot more than
  $50 million to become a neocloud."
- **$50M is not even $50M.** Only ~$5.25M is committed; ~$44.75M is at the
  investor's option. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.4.) The capital input to the loop is a trickle,
  not a flood ‚Äî and a single H100 is ~$30‚Äì40K, so committed capital buys a few
  hundred GPUs against CoreWeave's ~45,000+. (DCD, 2026; DOSSIER ¬ß4.)

**Supporting (the only positive loop energy available to NewBird):**

- The *industry-level* demand loop is real and strong: Big-4 hyperscaler 2026
  capex ~$660B (+77%); inference shifting to ~80% of neocloud by 2030. (DOSSIER
  ¬ß5.) But this is a tailwind to the *system*, captured by whoever sits at the
  reinforcing-loop's center ‚Äî and that is demonstrably not NewBird.

[JUDGMENT] NewBird is attempting to **board a violently positive reinforcing loop
from its losing end**, with neither of the loop's two ignition inputs (power,
capital-at-scale). The model predicts the loop will *amplify the gap*, not close
it: scale begets scale, and NewBird has none.

### System C ‚Äî The financing structure: an engineered reflexive *negative* loop

This is the cleanest, most dangerous loop in the whole situation, and it is
*designed* to be self-reinforcing in the wrong direction.

**Disconfirming for the equity holder** (cited):

- **The death-spiral convertible is a reflexive loop by construction.** Its
  "Alternate Conversion Price" = **93% of the lowest 10-day VWAP (85% over 15
  days on default)** ‚Äî the conversion price floats *down* with the stock. (DOSSIER
  ¬ß2.4.) The academic and practitioner literature names this exact instrument a
  "death spiral" precisely because of the loop: "the floating conversion rate
  tied to the lowest trading price ‚Ä¶ leads to catastrophic dilution as the stock
  falls, creating a negative feedback loop"; the holder can short, convert at the
  depressed price, cover, and repeat. (Wikipedia / ScienceDirect / Nasdaq on
  toxic financing, 2025.) The dossier states it plainly: **"dilution worsens as
  the equity weakens."** (DOSSIER ¬ß2.4.)
- **The loop has a control node that locks in the operator.** The investor holds
  a **24-month right to ‚â•55% of all future Company/NewBird financings** on the
  same terms. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.4, ¬ß8.) Every attempt to raise growth capital for
  System B re-feeds System C's dilution loop ‚Äî the two systems are *coupled*, and
  the coupling is adverse to common holders.
- **The market premium is itself a reflexive bubble loop.** The 4/15 pivot drove
  **+~582% intraday** ($6.82 ‚Üí $24.31 high) on narrative, then ‚àí25% to ‚àí36% the
  next day; ~16‚Äì18% short interest, ~87% realized vol around a binary vote. The
  explicit historical analog is **Long Island Iced Tea ‚Üí "Long Blockchain"**
  (rebrand ‚Üí surge ‚Üí delisting). (DOSSIER ¬ß7.) Narrative ‚Üí price spike ‚Üí more
  narrative is a reinforcing loop with no fundamental anchor; it is the kind that
  reverses violently when the catalyst clears.

**No offsetting positive financing loop exists.** There is no cash generation to
*break* the dilution loop from inside; the only brake is the asset-sale cash, 100%
of which is a mandatory redemption to the noteholder. (DOSSIER ¬ß2.4.)

## Reasoning

The Feedback Loops lens is **highly relevant** here ‚Äî arguably one of the more
illuminating lenses for this specific situation ‚Äî because the case is *defined* by
loop dynamics, just not favorable ones.

A Munger compounder is a business where the dominant loop is reinforcing and
virtuous. Run the test on each system and **all three loops point down**:

- **System A (legacy)** is a dominant, *accelerating* decay loop (revenue,
  margin, stores, cash all compounding downward, faster each period). It is not
  being reversed; it is being *amputated* via the $39M sale. The flywheel is
  already broken ‚Äî this is the "doom loop" in its terminal phase.
- **System B (NewBird)** must enter the single steepest reinforcing loop in the
  modern economy (GPUaaS scale ‚Üí power ‚Üí allocation ‚Üí backlog ‚Üí capital), and it
  enters with ~0% share and *neither ignition input*. The model's core teaching ‚Äî
  reinforcing loops amplify *whatever position you start from* ‚Äî predicts the
  scale gap *widens*. CoreWeave's $100B backlog and 1+ GW are the visible proof
  the loop's energy is going elsewhere. A sub-scale entrant on a positive
  reinforcing loop doesn't slowly converge; it gets *left behind faster*.
- **System C (financing)** is an *engineered* reflexive negative loop where
  weakness feeds dilution feeds weakness, coupled to System B via the 55%
  co-invest right so that growth attempts re-arm the dilution.

The honest synthesis: **the only loop with strong positive energy (the GPUaaS
demand/scale flywheel) is one Allbirds sits *outside* of, and the loops it sits
*inside* of are all turning down.** There is no virtuous reinforcing loop
operating *for* the BIRD common holder anywhere in the system.

**Check on our own bias (the inversion the contract demands).** Could I be
pattern-matching "death spiral" too eagerly? Two genuine counter-considerations:
(1) The *industry* loop is real and explosive ‚Äî if NewBird could somehow secure
power + an anchor tenant + non-toxic capital, it could in principle latch onto the
flywheel; small entrants do occasionally board it (this is why I do not call a
fatal flaw on *this* model). (2) Reflexivity cuts both ways: a 71%-locked "yes"
vote into a 16‚Äì18% short book could trigger a *positive* short-squeeze loop on the
stock ‚Äî but that is a price/liquidity loop, not a *business-value* loop, and it
would be transient. Neither counter-consideration changes the dominant-loop read;
they lower my certainty about the *timing and violence* of the moves, not the
*direction* of the underlying loops.

**Where the model is a stretch:** Feedback Loops is a lens on *operating systems*.
The cleanest read applies to the legacy business and the industry. For the
go-forward entity, I am reasoning about a loop NewBird has *not yet entered* ‚Äî so
System B is partly a prediction about a loop, not an observation of one. I weight
that honestly: high relevance, but the go-forward conclusion is inference, not
yet measured fact (the dossier flags the same gap, ¬ß9 item 3).

## What would flip this read (disconfirmers)

- **System B ignition.** NewBird secures (a) a real, contracted power allocation
  at meaningful MW *and* (b) a multi-year anchor tenant with disclosed economics
  (utilization, spread) *and* (c) non-toxic growth capital ‚Äî i.e., evidence it is
  actually *on* the reinforcing loop rather than outside it. Disclosed backlog
  beyond the single $2.75M QumulusAI lease would be the first data point.
- **System C neutralized.** The convertible is retired/refinanced into a
  fixed-conversion instrument, or the 55% co-invest right is waived ‚Äî removing the
  reflexive dilution loop. (None disclosed as of 2026-06-01; DOSSIER ¬ß9.)
- **Legacy loop reversal (now moot).** Sequential revenue *growth* with *stable*
  gross margin and positive FCF would indicate the decay loop had been arrested ‚Äî
  but the asset is being sold, so this is no longer achievable for BIRD holders.
- **Positive-energy redirection.** Credible evidence (a named cloud/data-center
  operator team, a signed power PPA) that the industry demand loop is being
  *captured* by NewBird rather than merely cited as a TAM tailwind.
