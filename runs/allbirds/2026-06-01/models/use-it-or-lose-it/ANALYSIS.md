# Use-It-or-Lose-It (Competence Atrophy) — Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD)

**Discipline:** Psychology  ·  **Intensity:** lens  ·  **As-of:** 2026-06-01

## What this model predicts

"Use it or lose it" says a capability decays when it stops being exercised. The
muscle metaphor: an organization that coasts on a legacy strength — and quietly
under-feeds the engine that built it (R&D, engineering talent, distribution
reach, the disciplines of acquiring and keeping customers) — loses that strength
faster and more permanently than the income statement reveals, because the *skill
base* erodes before the revenue does, and skill is far harder to rebuild than to
lose. Applied as a Munger lens it cuts two ways: (a) at the **subject** — is the
core competence atrophying while management lives off accumulated brand equity?
and (b) at the **forward business** — does it possess (or is it actively building)
the competence its strategy requires, or is it asserting a capability it does not
exercise?

If the naive atrophy thesis applied **favorably** to a footwear turnaround, we'd
expect to see the innovation muscle being *kept in use*: a credible cadence of new
product and materials, a staffed and led design/R&D function, and distribution
discipline. If it applied **adversely** (the bearish version of the model), we'd
expect: stalled product pipeline, gutted R&D and design talent, a hollowed
distribution network, and — for any pivot — a new strategy in a domain where the
firm has *no exercised competence at all*.

This is a "lens" assignment: reason over the dossier, confirm lightly externally,
and use the model as a check on our own reasoning too.

## Evidence

This is a two-entity question. The legacy footwear business and the surviving
"NewBird AI" shell give the model opposite readings, so I separate them.

### A. Legacy footwear competence — the atrophy thesis is WEAKER than it looks

**Disconfirming the "R&D atrophied" claim** (cited):
- Allbirds kept its **innovation muscle in use right up to the sale.** In 2025 it
  launched **Terralux with INNOVERA**, a bio-designed leather alternative from
  plant-based proteins, biopolymers, and recycled Nylon 6 from end-of-life tires —
  an industry-first, not a re-color (Allbirds IR press release, 2025; Trellis).
  It also shipped the **"Remix"** collection turning textile/foam waste into shoes
  via partners Blumaka and Circ (Allbirds IR; ESG Dive 2025), the **Kiwi
  Collection** (three new silhouettes), and a **refreshed Wool Runner / "remaster
  the icons"** program (WWD 2025-09; Retail Dive Q3-2025).
- Allbirds maintained a **dedicated product-development center in Vietnam set to
  deliver new concepts, materials and prototypes into the back half of 2025 and
  2026** (WWD 2025-09). The R&D function was funded and forward-loaded, not shut.
- The **design organization was rebuilt, not dismantled.** A newly created
  **Chief Design Officer** role was filled Dec-2023 by **Adrian Nyman** (13 years
  at Nike; ex-Chief Brand Officer at Arrival), and a **VP of Design** (Jason
  Israel, ex-global creative director at Salomon, ~2 decades at North Face/K2)
  joined June-2024 — i.e., the firm was *adding* footwear-design talent during the
  decline (Allbirds IR; Retail Dive 2024). [JUDGMENT: this is the opposite of a
  team being allowed to wither.]
- Capex was cut to maintenance-only (~$3.1M FY2025, ~2% of revenue; DOSSIER §2.2),
  but in footwear the binding investment is design/materials headcount, which was
  *increased*, not the tooling line — so low capex is weak evidence of R&D atrophy.

**Supporting the atrophy thesis on the dimensions that actually mattered** (cited):
- **Distribution muscle genuinely atrophied.** The store network collapsed from
  **60 stores (2024) to 23 (late-2025)**, with the **remaining US full-price
  stores closing by end-Feb-2026** (DOSSIER §1; Retail Dive). The DTC customer-
  acquisition and physical-retail competence — a real strength at the 2021 peak —
  was deliberately run off. Revenue fell **−48.8% from the FY2022 peak and a
  further −30.5% in Q1-2026** (DOSSIER §2.2).
- **The product's core quality edge eroded** even as new launches kept coming:
  merino-wool uppers were widely reported to pill, stain and lose structure within
  6–12 months (multiple 2025–26 retrospectives, e.g. The Spinoff 2026; product
  reviews). [JUDGMENT: the *durability* sub-competence — making the comfort claim
  stick — decayed, and competitors (On, Hoka) out-innovated on performance while
  Nike/Adidas/Puma commoditized the sustainability angle (DOSSIER §4).]
- **Recurring inventory write-downs every year 2022–2025** ($14.4M/$8.3M/$2.7M/
  $2.8M; DOSSIER §2.2) signal an atrophied *merchandising/demand-sensing* muscle —
  chronic over-buying of the wrong product — even while creative output continued.

[JUDGMENT] So the legacy story is **not** the textbook "coasting on a legacy brand
while starving R&D." The R&D/design engine was kept running and even re-staffed;
what atrophied was **commercial execution** — distribution reach, demand sensing,
and the durability that made the original product defensible. The innovation
output couldn't outrun a broken go-to-market. And critically for this lens, the
entire footwear competence base (brand, IP, the Vietnam development center,
inventory, contracts) is being **sold to AXNY for $39M** (DOSSIER §1) — so for the
investable security, this competence is **exiting the building entirely**, not
merely atrophying.

### B. NewBird AI competence — the model's most extreme adverse reading

**Supporting (cited):**
- The surviving public shell intends to become an **"Electronics Infrastructure
  Business"** buying NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs and leasing them, which the company's
  *own* risk factors call **"highly speculative, uncertain, unproven"** with
  **"no operating history"** (DOSSIER §1; Ex. 99.2 to 4/20/2026 8-K).
- **No AI/cloud/data-center competence exists or has been hired.** CEO Joe
  Vernachio and CFO Ann Mitchell have **no technology/cloud/data-center/GPU/
  enterprise-IT background**; **no AI/cloud executives or directors have been
  named** (DOSSIER §6). External coverage is blunt: "The company has not announced
  any hires with cloud infrastructure or AI experience... None of the people who
  ran a shoe company have obvious qualifications for any of these tasks" (DCD 2026;
  TheNextWeb 2026; KORE1 2026).
- The **only operating proof point** is a single **~$2.75M, 3-year GPU lease to a
  QumulusAI subsidiary** (DOSSIER §1, §8) — i.e., the firm has outsourced the one
  capability (running GPUs/orchestration) it claims to be entering, to its
  counterparty.
- The domain it is entering rewards *exercised* capital-and-power competence the
  shell lacks: the cost-advantaged winners are crypto-miner pivots (TeraWulf,
  IREN, Applied Digital) who win on **pre-existing gigawatt-scale power +
  capital** (DOSSIER §4). Constellation Research: NewBird "is going to need a lot
  more than $50 million to become a neocloud" (DOSSIER §4).

**Disconfirming / nuance (cited):**
- Strictly, "use it or lose it" describes the *decay of an existing* capability.
  NewBird does not have a GPU/cloud competence to lose — the capability is simply
  **absent from inception.** [JUDGMENT: that is arguably worse than atrophy, but it
  is a *different* failure mode (a capability-acquisition problem), which is why I
  hold this model's relevance to the forward entity at moderate rather than
  maximal — Circle of Competence / Lollapalooza / Incentives speak to it more
  directly than the atrophy lens does.]

### Independent research run for this lens (sources)
- Allbirds 2025 product/innovation cadence and Vietnam R&D center: WWD

- Design-org rebuild (Nyman CDO Dec-2023; Israel VP Design June-2024): Allbirds IR;

## Reasoning

**On the subject.** The atrophy lens looks for a firm living off a legacy strength
while quietly letting the engine rust. Allbirds only *half*-fits. The creative/
materials engine — the thing the brand was famous for — was genuinely **kept in
use and re-staffed** through 2025; that is real disconfirming evidence against a
lazy "they stopped innovating" narrative. But the *commercial* competences
(distribution reach, demand sensing, the durability that made the comfort claim
defensible) **did atrophy / commoditize**, and that is what killed the business.
The deeper Munger point lands anyway: a company can keep one muscle fit while the
muscles that actually monetize it waste away — and the market still pays the price.
Then the firm did the most decisive "lose it" act possible: it **sold the entire
competence base** (brand, IP, R&D center, team) to a licensing house. For a holder
of BIRD today, the legacy competence is not "atrophying" — it is *gone* at close.

**On the forward entity**, the lens reads strongly negative but slightly off-axis:
NewBird isn't losing a GPU/cloud muscle, it never had one and has hired no one who
does, while entering a domain that punishes the un-exercised. The model's verdict
(a firm operating where it has no live competence is structurally fragile) is
correct here; I simply note that "absence" is doing more work than "atrophy," so
sibling models (Circle of Competence, Incentive-Caused Bias, Lollapalooza) carry
the heavier weight and this lens should be down-weighted accordingly in synthesis.

**As a check on our own analysis (the model turned inward).** "Use it or lose it"
also warns the analyst: don't let a *narrative* muscle substitute for a *capability*
muscle. The danger here is twice over — (1) confusing Allbirds' visible innovation
press releases for a healthy business (the R&D output was real but the firm still
failed), and (2) confusing a rebrand and a $50M headline for an AI competence
(only ~$5.25M is committed and zero cloud talent exists; DOSSIER §2.4, §6). The
discipline is to score the *exercised* capability, not the asserted one. I have
tried to credit the genuine footwear innovation and refuse credit for the
non-existent AI capability.

**Calibration.** Relevance is moderate, not high: this lens is illuminating but is
*not* the sharpest tool for this situation — the legacy innovation engine partly
disconfirms the atrophy story, and the forward problem is better described as
capability-absence than capability-decay. Strength is moderate-to-high on the
distribution/commercial-atrophy and the AI-competence-vacuum facts. Conviction is
solid because the underlying facts (store count, talent roster, the sale, the
absence of any AI hire) are well-sourced.

## What would flip this read (disconfirmers)

- **NewBird hires a credible, named bench of GPU/cloud/data-center engineers and
  enterprise-sales leaders** (and discloses it) — would convert "no exercised
  competence" into "competence being built," materially softening the adverse
  forward read.
- **Disclosed NewBird operating traction beyond the single QumulusAI lease** —
  contracted backlog, utilization, in-house orchestration — showing a capability is
  actually being exercised rather than outsourced.
- **Evidence the legacy footwear R&D/design talent and Vietnam center are retained
  by the surviving entity** (rather than transferred to AXNY) — would mean the
  innovation muscle is not, in fact, leaving. (Current evidence: the brand/IP/assets
  go to AXNY, so this is unlikely; DOSSIER §1.)
- **On the legacy side, a counterfactual where the re-staffed design org + Terralux
  pipeline had been given time** — already moot given the sale, but it underscores
  that R&D atrophy was *not* the proximate cause of failure.

**Bottom line:** The naive "coasting on a dying brand while R&D rots" story does
**not** cleanly fit Allbirds: through 2025 the firm kept its materials/design
innovation muscle in active use and even re-staffed it (Terralux/INNOVERA, Remix,
a Nike/Salomon-pedigree design org, a Vietnam development center). What actually
atrophied was the *commercial* competence — distribution reach, demand sensing,
and the durability that made the product defensible — and that competence base is
now being **sold away entirely** to AXNY. On the forward "NewBird AI" entity the
lens reads sharply negative but slightly off-axis: the shell has **no exercised
GPU/cloud competence and has hired none**, so the failure mode is capability
*absence* rather than decay. Net: a genuinely mixed read with moderate relevance —
useful color, but a down-weighted voice that defers to Circle of Competence,
Incentives, and Inversion for the heavy verdict.
