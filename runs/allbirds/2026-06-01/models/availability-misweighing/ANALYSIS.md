# Availability-Misweighing — Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD)

**Discipline:** Psychology  ·  **Intensity:** standard  ·  **As-of:** 2026-06-01

## What this model predicts

Availability-misweighing (Tversky-Kahneman availability heuristic, as Munger uses it
in "The Psychology of Human Misjudgment") says people estimate probability and value
by how *easily vivid examples come to mind*, not by the boring statistical base rate.
The vivid, recent, emotionally charged data point crowds out the dull frequency table.

Applied to a *market price*, the model predicts: **if availability bias is driving the
quote, the price will reflect a recent, dramatic, easily-recalled story and will ignore
the unglamorous base rate for "this kind of situation."** Concretely, if this lens
applies, we should observe:

1. A **vivid, recent, narrative-laden event** the crowd is anchoring on (e.g., a giant
   one-day pop tied to a buzzword that is itself top-of-mind from other vivid winners).
2. A price that sits **above what the boring, well-documented base rate** for this exact
   setup (micro-cap rebrand-pivots; death-spiral convertibles; sub-scale neoclouds;
   asset-sale shells) would justify.
3. **Under-weighting of dull, knowable arithmetic** — here, the ~$1.34/sh cash dividend,
   the fairness-opinion range, and the arms-length bid — because spreadsheets are not
   "available" the way a 582% green candle is.
4. Commentators **explicitly reaching for the nearest vivid analog** (a sign the *whole
   market* is reasoning by availability, not base rates).

The model is **bidirectional**, so it also forces a check on *our own* bias: am I, the
analyst, over-weighting the vivid "Long Blockchain to fraud to zero" cautionary tale and
under-weighting the boring fact that a real $39M cash sale with a 71%-locked vote
provides genuine near-term downside backing?

## Evidence

**Supporting** (the market IS misweighing availability — price > base rate):

- **The vivid event is textbook-available.** The 4/15/2026 NewBird AI pivot drove
  **+~582% intraday** ($6.82 open to $24.31 high), then **-25% to -36%** the next day,
  with a 4/17 volatility halt. The stock still trades **~$4.18**, well above pre-pivot
  ~$2.69. (Dossier §7; Bloomberg 2026-04-16; CNBC 2026-04-15.) A 582% candle tied to the
  single most-available buzzword of the cycle ("AI/GPU") is the most "available" object
  imaginable.
- **The boring base rate for THIS setup is grim and well-documented.** The closest
  reference class — a small, struggling consumer company that bolts a hot-sector word
  onto its name — has a brutal base rate. **Long Island Iced Tea to "Long Blockchain"
  (Dec-2017): +500% on the rename, Nasdaq delisted it in 2018 for misleading investors,
  the SEC revoked registration in Feb-2021, and insider-trading charges followed; the
  blockchain business was never actually built.** (Wikipedia; CoinDesk 2021-02-22; CNN
  2021-07-10; Baker McKenzie 2021-02-24.) Multiple credible outlets reach for *exactly
  this analog* for BIRD (Sherwood, Motley Fool, IBKR's Sosnick — Dossier §7), and one AI
  outlet now calls the "**NewBird AI playbook**" a *reproducible* meme expected to recur
  in retail/hospitality/media within 12 months (Medium/IndexBox 2026). When the crowd
  needs a vivid analog to reason, it is reasoning by availability.
- **Sub-scale neocloud base rate is "unlikely to survive."** The reference class for the
  *go-forward* business is equally unglamorous: of 100+ neoclouds, only **10–15 operate
  at meaningful US scale** and "**only a handful will survive**"; initial exits expected
  Q1–Q2 2026; post-depreciation gross margins **14–16%**, capital recovery in 48–60
  months, GPU prices -50%/5yr. Vultr explicitly concludes small-scale survival is
  "unlikely"; Constellation says NewBird "is going to need a lot more than $50 million."
  (Dossier §4–5; Vultr 2026-01-28; McKinsey 2026; ABI 2026.) NewBird is **below the
  rated universe** — ~4 orders of magnitude below CoreWeave.
- **Death-spiral convertible base rate = "severe post-issuance wealth destruction."**
  The financing is a classic toxic/death-spiral convertible (sub-VWAP floating
  conversion, 5% OID, 12% coupon, 25% premiums, investor short-selling incentive). The
  documented base rate: continuous conversion to dilution to lower price to more
  conversion, "often leading to bankruptcy," with full $50M giving the investor **~72.9%
  of Class A**. (Dossier §2.4; Wikipedia/Nasdaq on death-spiral financing.)
- **The dull arithmetic is being under-weighted.** The boring, knowable anchors all sit
  *far below* $4.18: fairness-opinion per-share **$0.02–$1.83**; arms-length whole-
  company bid **$2.00–$2.25**; Board's own dividend estimate **~$1.34/sh**; triangulated
  intrinsic mid **~$1.40–$1.55**. Price is **above the high end of the company's own
  fairness range and above the best arms-length bid** — a ~-86% to -199% margin of
  safety. (Dossier §3.) The reverse-DCF says the market is paying **$20–28M** for a
  business that is *one* ~$2.75M GPU lease. The crowd is weighting the available story,
  not the available spreadsheet.

**Disconfirming** (the boring base rate is NOT entirely ignored — partial efficiency):

- **The price has already decayed ~83% from the vivid peak** ($24.31 to ~$4.18). The
  availability premium is *deflating in real time*: the stock was below $6 by mid-May as
  "skepticism" grew (Bloomberg; primaryignition 2026-05-10). Pure availability would
  hold the price near the vivid high; instead the boring base rate is visibly clawing it
  back. So the misweighing is *partial*, not total — and shrinking.
- **There IS a real, boring cash anchor on the downside.** Unlike Long Blockchain (which
  had no cash event), BIRD has a **signed $39M APA**, a **71%-locked vote** (Support
  Agreements), and a Board-estimated **~$1.34/sh** dividend with a 2026-05-20 record
  date. That is a genuine, near-term, knowable cash return — the dull fact that *limits*
  how far availability can push the price down, too. This disconfirms a naive "it's
  Long Blockchain, ergo zero" reading and is the check on *my own* availability bias.
- **Short interest ~16–18% of float** shows part of the crowd is betting *against* the
  story — the base-rate skeptics are present and sizeable, so the price is not a
  one-sided availability bubble. (Dossier §7.)

**Independent research run for this model** (cited inline above): base rates for
(a) name-change-to-hot-sector pivots [Long Blockchain: Wikipedia, CoinDesk 2021-02-22,
CNN 2021-07-10]; (b) death-spiral convertible outcomes [Wikipedia/Nasdaq on death-spiral
financing]; (c) sub-scale neocloud survival [Vultr 2026-01-28, McKinsey 2026, ABI 2026];
(d) the post-spike decay path [Bloomberg 2026-04-16, Fast Company, primaryignition
2026-05-10]. The "NewBird AI playbook is reproducible" framing is from Medium/IndexBox
(2026) and is itself *evidence of availability cascade*, not a base rate I rely on.

## Reasoning

**On the subject (the market).** This is close to a pure-play case for the model. The
price is anchored to the single most vivid, recent, emotionally salient event available
(a 582% AI pop), while three separate, well-documented, *boring* base rates all point the
same way down: rebrand-pivots usually fail (Long Blockchain), sub-scale neoclouds usually
die (Vultr/McKinsey), and death-spiral convertibles usually destroy equity (Nasdaq/Wiki).
The clincher is the **disconnect between the available story and the available
arithmetic**: the cash math (~$1.34/sh dividend, $1.83 fairness high, $2.25 bid) is
*equally knowable* and *more reliable* than the AI narrative, yet the price ignores it by
2–3x. Munger's point is precisely that the dull, statistically correct anchor loses to
the vivid one in the mind — and that is what the $20–28M "AI premium" over disclosed
asset value is.

A strong confirmation of *market-level* availability reasoning: credible commentators do
not build a model — they **reach for the nearest vivid analog** (Long Blockchain) on both
sides of the trade. When the marginal price-setter reasons by analogy to a salient story
rather than by base rate, availability is the operative mechanism.

**On our own analysis (the bidirectional check).** The model warns *me* too. The Long
Blockchain to fraud to delisting story is itself extremely available and could seduce me
into scoring this a fatal-flaw zero. I deliberately down-weight that: BIRD differs in the
one way that matters to downside — it has a **signed cash sale and a locked vote**, so the
boring base rate here is not "$0 like Long Blockchain" but "**~$1.34/sh of fairly-likely
cash plus a near-worthless option.**" The correct base-rate-anchored read is therefore not
"fraud/zero" but "**price is ~2–3x the defensible cash-backed value because the crowd is
paying an availability premium for the AI option.**" That is a *negative* signal on the
*price* without being a *fatal* judgment on the *asset*. Availability-misweighing is not a
fatal-capable model (it is a price/behavior lens, not incentives/inversion/accounting/
margin-of-safety), so `fatalFlaw = false` regardless of how grim the picture looks.

**Where the model is a stretch.** Availability speaks to *why the price is wrong*, not to
*what the business is worth* — that is the job of the valuation/inversion/competition
lenses. And the misweighing is **partial and already correcting** (83% off the peak), so
the lens is less powerful at $4.18 than it was at $24.31. It tells synthesis: "the
residual premium over cash-backing is a behavioral artifact that is decaying," which is
useful but not, by itself, a full thesis.

## What would flip this read (disconfirmers)

- **Price re-converges to the boring anchor.** If BIRD trades down to ~$1.34–$1.83 (the
  dividend/fairness range), the availability premium has been arbitraged away and the
  signal weakens to neutral — the boring base rate would have *won*.
- **A signed, sizeable, credit-worthy GPU backlog** (well beyond the one ~$2.75M
  QumulusAI lease) would convert the "vivid story" into a real cash-flow base rate, in
  which case the price would no longer be an availability artifact but a discounted
  forward — flipping the signal toward neutral/mixed.
- **The 6/3 vote fails or the asset sale breaks**, removing the boring cash anchor.
  Paradoxically this would *strengthen* the "availability-driven overvaluation" read on
  the downside (no cash backing, pure story) — i.e., it confirms rather than flips, but
  it changes the magnitude.
- **Evidence that base rates here are genuinely different** — e.g., NewBird secures
  gigawatt-scale power + cheap capital (the only things that let crypto-miner pivots like
  IREN/TeraWulf actually win). Absent that, the reference class holds. (Dossier §4.)

**Bottom line:** This is a near-archetypal availability-misweighing setup: the price is
anchored to a vivid, recent, easily-recalled 582% AI pop while three independent, dull,
well-documented base rates — rebrand-pivots (Long Blockchain), sub-scale neoclouds
("unlikely to survive"), and death-spiral convertibles ("severe wealth destruction") —
all point down, and the equally-knowable cash arithmetic (~$1.34/sh dividend vs $4.18
price) is being ignored by 2–3x. The lens reads the residual premium as a behavioral
artifact, not a fundamentally supported value, so the signal is clearly **negative** on
the *price*. It is not fatal: a real signed $39M sale and a 71%-locked vote provide
genuine downside cash backing, which is also the discipline's check on *my own*
temptation to over-weight the vivid "fraud/zero" analog. The premium is already decaying
(83% off the peak), so the misweighing is partial and self-correcting.
