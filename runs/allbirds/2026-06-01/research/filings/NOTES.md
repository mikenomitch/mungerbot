# SEC Filings — Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD)

**Lane:** filings · **As-of:** 2026-06-01 · **Specialist:** SEC Filings research
**Issuer:** Allbirds, Inc. · **CIK** 0001653909 · **Exchange/Ticker:** Nasdaq: BIRD (Class A common, $0.0001 par)
**State of incorporation:** Delaware (a **public benefit corporation**, B-Corp certified) · **SIC** 2300 (Apparel) · **FY end** 12/31
**Auditor:** Deloitte & Touche LLP, San Francisco (auditor since 2017) · **Filer status:** smaller reporting / emerging growth company
**Primary source throughout:** SEC EDGAR. Filing index artifact: `artifacts/filings-index.md`. Financial table: `artifacts/filings-financial-summary.csv`.

> **THE HEADLINE — read this first.** As of the as-of date, Allbirds is **not operating as a going-concern footwear company in any normal sense.** It has signed an agreement to **sell substantially all of its footwear business and the "Allbirds" brand** for **$39M cash**, and is **simultaneously attempting a radical pivot** of the surviving public shell into a **GPU/compute-infrastructure leasing business ("NewBird AI")** funded by a **$50M toxic convertible note**. The auditor has issued a **going-concern qualification**. A special-meeting vote on the asset sale + dissolution + charter changes is pending (originally scheduled May 18, 2026; lock-up support agreements cover ~71% of voting power). This is a distressed, event-driven situation, not an operating-business analysis.

## 1. The transformational transaction — "Project Arise" (the single most important fact set)

### 1a. Asset sale (signed 3/29/2026)
Source: 8-K/DEFA14A filed 2026-03-31, *Item 1.01*, "Entry into Asset Purchase Agreement" (`allbirdsprojectarisepr8-k.htm`, event date 3/29/2026).

- On **March 29, 2026**, Allbirds, Inc. (Seller) and **Allbirds IP LLC** — "a Delaware limited liability company **affiliated with American Exchange Group**" (Purchaser) — entered an **Asset Purchase Agreement** under which Purchaser will acquire **"substantially all of the Seller's assets, including those related to intellectual property assets (including global trademarks, trade names, copyrights, patents, domain names, social media accounts, customer lists, and related IP), inventory, wherever located, accounts receivable, assigned contracts, including license and franchise agreements, transition assets, prepaid expenses, goodwill and other assets."** (Quoted.)
- **Purchase price: $39 million in cash** at closing, "subject to standard purchase price adjustments… and less the Escrow Fund." (Quoted.)
- **$2 million deposit** into escrow within 2 business days; credited at closing.
- **$3 million Escrow Fund** held at closing = primary recourse for negative price adjustments and breaches of "Surviving Representations" (survive 60 days post-closing).
- Negotiated/approved by a **special committee of independent directors**; unanimous full-Board approval. **Conditioned on stockholder approval.**
- **No-shop** with fiduciary-out for a "Superior Proposal."
- **Outside date: June 30, 2026**, extendable up to two 30-day periods if SEC has not finished reviewing the proxy.
- **Termination fee: $1.25 million** payable by the Company if it accepts a Superior Proposal (deposit returned to Purchaser).
- **Net proceeds estimate: ~$35.0M–$36.2M** after transaction expenses and taxes, assuming the $39.0M price (Source: Exhibit 99.2 supplemental risk factors to 4/20/2026 8-K — quoted). Subject to further reduction for escrow claims/contingent liabilities.

### 1b. Dissolution intent (the original plan)
Source: same 8-K, *Item 8.01, "Other Information."*
- The Board approved "**taking steps to effect the dissolution of the Company**." If stockholders approve the asset sale + dissolution, the Company "**intends to file a Certificate of Dissolution… with… Delaware**," then has **three years to wind up its affairs.**
- A **Plan of Distribution** would establish a **contingency reserve** and authorize distributions of any residual proceeds to stockholders. **Explicit hedge (quoted):** "there can be no assurance as to the timing and amount of distributions to the Company's stockholders, **if any**."

### 1c. The PIVOT — "Amended Business Plan" supersedes pure dissolution (announced mid-April 2026)
Source: 8-K filed 2026-04-20, *Item 8.01 "Amended Business Plan"* (`d34261d8k.htm`); press release 4/15/2026.
- Management states (quoted): "**We have been operating these footwear assets at a material loss and do not believe that continuing to operate these footwear assets is sustainable or beneficial to our stockholders.**"
- After closing, the buyer owns the "Allbirds" tradename and all related IP; **"the brand and footwear business will continue under the stewardship of the Buyer."**
- Allbirds, Inc. (the public shell) "**intend[s] to continue operating Allbirds, Inc. under a new corporate name**" and to enter the **"Electronics Infrastructure Business"**: "the **acquisition and monetization of graphics processing units, related high-performance computing infrastructure**… and other related assets" ("Electronics Assets"). Intended structures: "**sales, leases, sale/lease-back transactions and other monetization structures… capital-efficient and scalable, with customers bearing substantially all operating, maintenance, and infrastructure costs.**" (Quoted.)
- So the company chose to **NOT dissolve but reinvent itself** as a GPU-leasing vehicle. The dissolution remains the fallback if the pivot/financing fails.

### 1d. The financing for the pivot — $50M convertible notes ("the Facility")
Source: 8-K filed 2026-04-20, *Item 1.01 "Facility Matters."* Original Securities Purchase Agreement 4/14/2026; **Amended & Restated 4/19/2026**.
- **Senior secured convertible notes**, aggregate original principal **up to $50.0 million**, with **one institutional investor**, convertible into Class A common.
- Proceeds primarily to fund the purchase of Electronics Assets by **NewBird AI, LLC** ("NewBird Subsidiary"), a newly formed wholly owned subsidiary.
- **Tranching:** initial $5.25M ($3.25M at initial closing + $2.0M after Nasdaq Proposal approval); remaining up to **$44.75M at the Investor's option.**
- **Terms (quoted/summarized):** 24-month maturity; **12.0% coupon** (payable in stock or cash); **5% original issue discount**; **17% late charge**; secured by Electronics Assets + equity in NewBird (and, after the asset sale, all other Company assets).
- **Conversion mechanics (death-spiral features):** Conversion Price = 120% of a reference bid price; **"Alternate Conversion Price" = lower of conversion price and 93% of the lowest 10-day VWAP (85% over 15 days if an event of default exists)**; redemption/default premiums of **25%**; **4.99% beneficial-ownership blocker** (can step to 9.99%). Mandatory redemption of 25% of subsequent-placement proceeds and 100% of asset-sale proceeds (other than the Asset Sale).
- Investor gets a **24-month co-investment right** for ≥55% of any future Company financings on the same terms; plus a Registration Rights Agreement (4/19/2026).
- **Nasdaq Proposal:** stockholders are asked to approve issuance of >19.99% of shares on conversion (Nasdaq Rule 5635(d)). Preliminary proxy filed 4/14/2026.
- *Judgment (flagged as judgment):* this is a classic dilutive/"toxic" convertible typical of distressed micro-caps; the floating sub-VWAP conversion plus 4.99% blocker can drive substantial, ongoing share issuance and price pressure. Not a fact about value — a structural observation for downstream lanes.

### 1e. First GPU transaction (the only operating evidence for the new business)
Source: 8-K filed 2026-04-20, *Item 1.01 "GPU Lease."*
- Using initial-tranche proceeds, the Company (via the NewBird subsidiary / "Lessor") **escrowed funds to purchase server equipment using current-generation NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs** ("Purchased GPU Assets").
- Simultaneously entered an **~$2.75 million, three-year lease** with **a subsidiary of QumulusAI, Inc.** (the "Lessee"), with an end-of-term purchase option. **"This lease agreement represents the Company's first transaction within its new business line and marks the initial step towards executing its pivot into the compute infrastructure space."** (Quoted.)
- *Scale note:* a single ~$2.75M, 3-year lease is the entire operating track record of the "Electronics Infrastructure Business" as of the as-of date.

### 1f. Vote lock-up — Support Agreements
Source: 8-K filed 2026-04-15, *Item 1.01.*
- On **April 8, 2026**, the Company signed **Support Agreements with stockholders holding ~71% of voting power** (as of 2/28/2026), including **entities affiliated with Maveron** (>5% Class B holder), **Joey Zwillinger, Tim Brown, and Dick Boyce** (all directors). They agreed to vote as the Board recommends on all special-meeting proposals.
- *Implication (judgment):* with ~71% locked up and the dual-class structure (Class B = 10 votes), stockholder approval of the asset sale is effectively assured; minority Class A holders have little ability to block.

### 1g. Special-meeting timing & the DEFINITIVE proxy (DEFM14A filed 5/8/2026)
Sources: 8-K 4/20/2026 *Item 8.01*; **DEFM14A filed 2026-05-08** (`d39753ddefm14a.htm`, accession 0001193125-26-213226); PRER14A 5/4/2026.
- The Special Meeting was **rescheduled to June 3, 2026 at 12:00 p.m. PT** (originally 5/18/2026); record date **April 13, 2026**.
- ⚠️ **TIMING — critical for the as-of date:** **June 3, 2026 is TWO DAYS AFTER the 2026-06-01 as-of date.** Therefore, **as of 6/1/2026 the stockholder vote had NOT occurred and the asset sale was NOT yet approved or closed.** No Item 5.07 (vote results) 8-K and no closing 8-K appear in EDGAR through the last pre-as-of filing (5/28/2026). The 5/28 Third Amendment to the credit facility (adding term loans, see §6a) is consistent with the deal **not yet having closed**.
- **The four proposals (DEFM14A):**
  - **Proposal 1 — Asset Sale Proposal:** requires the affirmative vote of a **majority of outstanding common stock**.
  - **Proposal 2 — Charter Amendment Proposal:** requires **≥66 2/3% of total voting power** (this is the corporate rename + charter changes enabling the go-forward entity).
  - **Proposal 3 — Nasdaq Proposal:** majority of voting power present — approves issuance of >19.99% of shares on conversion of the convertible notes (Nasdaq Rule 5635(d)).
  - **Proposal 4 — Adjournment Proposal.**
- Given the ~71% Support-Agreement lock-up and Class B super-voting, passage is highly likely but **unconfirmed as of as-of date**.

### 1h. THE KEY NUMBER — estimated Asset Sale Dividend ≈ $1.34/share
Source: **DEFM14A (5/8/2026)** (quoted): "**The Board currently anticipates that the Asset Sale Dividend may be approximately $1.34 per share of common stock (based on 6,220,796 shares of Class A common stock and 2,540,381 shares of Class B common stock outstanding on the Record Date).**"
- Total share base ≈ **8.76M shares**. The dividend "**may vary significantly from this estimate, positively or negatively**," depending on asset-sale profit realization, recovery of deposits/cash collateral/prepaids, and known/unknown liabilities and contingencies; payable after closing and release of the Escrow Fund "if any."
- **Context for valuation lanes (facts):** the Board's ~$1.34/share estimated cash-to-holders compares to (i) the buyer's earlier floated price "equivalent to **$2.00 to $2.25 per share**" (American Exchange Group, advised by TD Cowen — proxy "Background of the Asset Sale"), and (ii) the **~$2.69** trading level when the CEO/CFO sold in early March 2026. The estimated dividend is **below** both. The difference reflects transaction costs, debt repayment (~$17.4M ABL paid in full at closing), taxes, and contingency reserves.
- **Fairness opinion:** delivered by the **Duff & Phelps Opinion Practice of Kroll, LLC** — oral opinion **March 27, 2026**, to the Special Committee and the Board (subsequently confirmed in writing). The Company engaged a financial advisor; a contingent advisory fee (~$2.0M, payable on consummation) plus expense reimbursement/indemnity is disclosed.
- ⚠️ Note: the surviving entity would also retain the speculative GPU/"Electronics Infrastructure Business" + the $50M convertible — so a Class A holder who does not sell into the market receives (a) the ~$1.34 cash dividend (if paid) **plus** (b) a residual interest in a renamed, pre-revenue, heavily-converted GPU-leasing shell. The proxy is explicit there is "no assurance" the go-forward business provides any value.

## 2. Business description & segments (from FY2025 10-K, filed 3/31/2026)

Source: 10-K FY2025, *Item 1. Business* (`bird-20251231.htm`).

- **What it is (legacy):** "**Allbirds is a global lifestyle brand that innovates with naturally derived materials to make better footwear and apparel products**." Founded 2015; Delaware **public benefit corporation**; **B-Corp certified since 2016**. Materials: ZQ-certified merino wool, tree fibers, sugarcane. "**Footwear is the foundation of our brand and represents the majority of our revenue**"; apparel (tees, sweats, socks, underwear) is secondary.
- **Channels:** "digitally-led vertical retail distribution" — own eCommerce + third-party marketplace + a small physical retail fleet + international third-party distributors.
- **Retail footprint:** **23 company-operated stores** in the US and UK at FY2025 (majority US). **Closed 10 stores in 2025** (9 US, 1 UK) and **15 US stores in 2024** — continued contraction.
- **Segments:** "**We operate as one operating segment.**" CODM = CEO; decisions made on consolidated data; no change in 2025 (10-K Note 8). Geographic disaggregation provided (US is the dominant market; "no individual foreign country contributed in excess of 10% of net revenue" in Q1 2026 per 10-Q).
- **International reliance:** uses third-party distributors internationally — flagged as a risk to results and brand value.

*Note:* the FY2025 10-K was filed two days after the asset sale was signed, so Item 1 still describes the footwear company; the transaction is carried in **Note 16 Subsequent Events** and as a risk factor.

## 4. Risk factors

### 4a. Legacy 10-K risk-factor summary (FY2025 10-K, *Item 1A*)
Top-line risks (quoted bullets): significant net losses since inception and continuing; **substantial doubt about going concern**; need for additional capital that may dilute; long-term growth-strategy execution; customer acquisition/retention; volatile results; reliance on **third-party international distributors**; discretionary-spend/economic sensitivity; **sustainable-materials cost pressure**; **manufacturer/supplier concentration**; limited retail footprint and commercial-real-estate risk; failed returns on store/operations investments; **ESG/sustainability-target reputational risk**; supplier-code-of-conduct compliance; raw-material cost volatility; Nasdaq listing maintenance.

### 4b. NEW / supplemental risk factors for the transaction & pivot (Exhibit 99.2 to 4/20/2026 8-K)
Three new clusters (these did not exist before 2026):

**(i) Risk Factors Relating to the Asset Sale** — *flagged NEW:*
- If the asset sale fails, the Company "may remain responsible for operating a legacy business that we have described as **unsustainable and loss-making**," with fewer strategic alternatives and a possible need to finance on unfavorable terms or dissolve.
- **Net proceeds uncertainty:** ~$35.0M–$36.2M expected, reducible by escrow claims/unforeseen liabilities; even with an "**Asset Sale Dividend**," remaining assets "may be insufficient to fund our continuing business, satisfy public company costs… or provide stockholders with any value in the go-forward business." (Quoted.)
- No-shop limits alternatives; escrow/contingent liabilities could cut distributions.
- **Conflicts of interest:** directors/officers "may have interests… in addition to, or different from, their interests as stockholders," including **concentrated voting control** (Class B 10 votes/share held by directors/affiliates).

**(ii) Risk Factors Relating to Our Anticipated Electronics Infrastructure Business** — *flagged NEW; unusually candid:*
- "**We have no operating history in the anticipated Electronics Infrastructure Business.**"
- "**Our Electronics Infrastructure Business is highly speculative, uncertain, unproven and subject to change, and we may be unable to implement it successfully or at all.**" "at a very early stage… plans remain preliminary and may change materially." (Quoted.)
- "We may fail to **transition successfully from a consumer products company to a computing infrastructure company**" — different sourcing, leasing, residual-value, hosting, technical and regulatory capabilities required.
- Disclosures "**necessarily involve substantial estimates and assumptions and may become inaccurate.**" Risk of reputational harm/litigation/regulatory scrutiny if disclosures are seen as overly optimistic.
- (Section continues with additional GPU-business risks — financing dependence, utilization/residual-value, counterparty concentration, technology obsolescence; full text in Exhibit 99.2.)

**(iii) Risk Factors Relating to the Charter Amendment Proposal** — *flagged NEW* (corporate rename/charter changes; full terms a DATA GAP).

## 6. Debt, off-balance-sheet & related-party items

### 6a. Credit facility (the binding constraint)
Source: 10-K *Item 7 / Note 6*; 8-Ks 3/31, 4/20, 5/28 2026.
- **$50.0M secured asset-backed revolving credit facility with Second Avenue Capital Partners LLC**, entered **June 30, 2025** ("Credit Agreement"). Borrowing-base / ABL structure; restrictive covenants (limits on debt, liens, mergers, asset sales, acquisitions, investments, restricted payments). **$17.4M drawn** at both 12/31/2025 and 3/31/2026 (shown as long-term debt). *Second Avenue is an asset-based/distressed-focused lender — consistent with a stressed credit.*
- **Amendments (rapid succession in 2026):**
  - **First Amendment (3/29/2026):** consents to the asset sale; lowers minimum Unrestricted Cash trigger (cash-dominion) from $10.0M to $7.5M; raises baskets; **replaces the Minimum Consolidated EBITDA covenant with a minimum Consolidated Liquidity covenant**; extends FY2025 financials delivery to 4/15/2026; requires obligations **paid in full in cash and revolving commitments terminated by the earlier of 10 days post-vote or June 19, 2026.**
  - **Second Amendment (4/19/2026):** permits the convertible-note Facility and liens on Electronics Assets to the Investor; with a **Subordination Agreement** subordinating the convertible Investor's claims/liens to the Agent (except on NewBird equity).
  - **Third Amendment (5/26/2026):** **reduces Revolving Commitments from $50M to $44.2M**; adds **Term Loan A ($3.3M max)** and **Term Loan B ($2.5M max)** tranches. *Adding small term loans while winding down indicates continued near-term cash need.*
