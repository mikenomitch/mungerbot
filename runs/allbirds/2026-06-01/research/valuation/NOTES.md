## Headline

BIRD trades at **$4.18** (2026-06-01 close) — **above the top of every disclosed intrinsic-value anchor**. The company's own fairness opinion (Duff & Phelps/Kroll) pegs per-share value at **$0.02–$1.83**; an arms-length whole-company bid was **$2.00–$2.25**. Margin of safety is **negative (~−86% to −199%)**. The premium is a speculative bet on an unproven "NewBird AI" GPU pivot, against going-concern doubt and a death-spiral convertible.

## Why this is not a normal valuation (method note)

Standard multiples and a forward earnings DCF do **not** apply:
- The footwear business is being **sold** (definitive APA, $39.0M, to American Exchange Group) and **exited**; legacy results are discontinued.
- The residual entity ("NewBird AI") is an AI-compute shell with **no operating history** — a single ~$2.75M GPU lease.
- Earnings, EBIT, and FCF are all **negative** ⇒ P/E, EV/EBIT, EV/FCF are n/m.

Method used: **special-situation sum-of-parts** = distributable asset value (the "Asset Sale Dividend") + option value of the NewBird AI stub, anchored to (a) the company's banker liquidation analysis and (b) an arms-length acquisition bid.

## Reverse-DCF read

Price implies the market values the un-launched NewBird AI business at roughly **$20M** (price ~$36.6M minus D&P-high asset value ~$16.9M) — to ~$28M vs. the D&P midpoint. That requires believing a single-lease, no-history GPU shell, funded by a dilutive convertible, is already worth multiples of its lone ~$2.75M asset. Not supported by any disclosed contracted revenue, backlog, or unit economics.

## Sensitivity (largest factors)

- **IP recovery** drives almost all equity value (heavy claims senior to equity): IP $30M ⇒ ~$0.02/sh; $35M ⇒ ~$0.92/sh; $40M ⇒ ~$1.83/sh. A $10M IP move ≈ $1.80/sh.
- **Special-dividend split:** each $9.2M distributed ≈ $1.00/sh of dividend; retained cash shifts value into the riskier NewBird stub.
- Discount-rate/terminal-growth sensitivity n/m (no cash-flow stream); the IP DCF already embeds royalty 4–6% / tax 28% / g 3%.

## Red flags (valuation-relevant)

- Trades **above** the company's own fairness-opinion high ($1.83) and the arms-length bid ($2.25) — no margin of safety, negative.
- **Going-concern** doubt + negative net cash pre-sale.
- **Death-spiral convertible** (93%-of-VWAP alternate conversion) → dilution worsens as price falls; full conversion gives investor ~73% of Class A.
- Fairness opinion **excludes insiders** (Maveron, Zwillinger, Brown) — fairness asserted only for non-affiliated holders.
- Extreme price instability (reportedly ~$21M → ~$148M market cap in one day on the AI-pivot news); 52-wk range $2.15–$24.31. Single-day quotes are fragile.
- ~99% value destruction from the ~$4.1B 2021 IPO valuation.

## Data gaps (explicit)

1. **Exact special-dividend per-share amount and the dividend-vs-retained split** — undisclosed (board discretion). Largest single determinant of common-holder value.
2. **Pro-forma post-close balance sheet** (cash retained, residual liabilities, escrow holdback size) not separately disclosed; D&P table is a liquidation proxy, not a pro-forma.
3. **Final IP sale-price realization** within the $30–40M range — sets equity value due to leverage.
4. **NewBird AI economics** — GPU lease spreads, utilization, contracted backlog beyond the one ~$2.75M QumulusAI lease: none disclosed; stub value is a judgment.
5. **Whether/when the optional $44.75M convertible is drawn** and at what VWAP-linked price — governs ultimate dilution.
6. **Vote outcome (June 3, 2026)** — Support Agreements exist (signed 2026-04-08) but the result is post-as-of-date.
