# DOSSIER — Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD) — as of 2026-06-01

> **One-paragraph orientation.** Allbirds is a going-concern-qualified footwear
> micro-cap (FY2025 net revenue $152.5M, net loss $77.3M) in the middle of
> dismantling itself. On 2026-03-29 it signed an Asset Purchase Agreement to sell
> **substantially all assets — including the "Allbirds" brand and global IP — to an
> American Exchange Group affiliate for $39M cash** ("Project Arise"), then in
> mid-April reframed the surviving public shell as **"NewBird AI"**, a speculative
> NVIDIA-GPU / compute-leasing venture funded by a **$50M toxic (death-spiral)
> convertible** (only ~$5.25M committed). The decisive stockholder vote is
> **June 3, 2026 — two days AFTER this as-of date — so the deal is APPROVED-PENDING,
> not consummated.** A Class A holder at the $4.18 price is paying for (a) an
> estimated **~$1.34/share** of cash (Board estimate, "may vary significantly")
> plus (b) a residual stake in a pre-revenue GPU shell whose only operating asset
> is a single **~$2.75M GPU lease to a QumulusAI subsidiary**. The price sits
> **above every disclosed intrinsic anchor** (fairness-opinion high $1.83; whole-
> company bid high $2.25). [JUDGMENT: this is a special-situation / event-driven
> shell, not an operating-business investment.]

## 1. Business & Segments

**What it is (legacy, being sold).** Allbirds, Inc. designs and sells sustainability-
positioned footwear and apparel, historically direct-to-consumer (DTC) plus wholesale.
**Single reportable segment**; the only disclosed split is geography — **US ~78% /
International ~22%** of FY2025 revenue (US $118.6M, Intl $33.8M).
Physical footprint shrank hard: **60 stores (2024) → 23 company-operated stores
(US+UK) by Q3/late 2025**, after closing 10 in 2025 and 15 in 2024.
(Source: FY2025 10-K, filed 2026-03-31; financial-summary.csv. The DTC-vs-wholesale
split is no longer a reported line — "~70–75% DTC" is third-party commentary, not
filed data.)

**The transformational event — "Project Arise" (asset sale).** On **2026-03-29**
Allbirds signed an **Asset Purchase Agreement** to sell *substantially all* assets —
IP / global trademarks / **the "Allbirds" brand**, inventory, accounts receivable,
contracts, goodwill — to **Allbirds IP LLC, an affiliate of American Exchange Group
(AXNY)**, for **$39.0M cash** (estimated net proceeds **~$35.0M–$36.2M**).
Negotiated by a Special Committee; unanimous Board approval; subject to a stockholder
vote; **$1.25M termination fee** with a superior-proposal fiduciary out; AXNY posted a
**$2M escrow deposit**. Advisors: TD Cowen (financial), Holland & Hart (legal),
Duff & Phelps Opinion Practice of Kroll (fairness opinion).
(Sources: 8-K / DEFA14A 2026-03-31, Item 1.01; DEFM14A 2026-05-08.)
AXNY is a licensing/accessories house (~30 brands incl. Aerosoles, Ed Hardy,
Ecko Unltd.) that **will license the Allbirds name** — i.e., the brand survives as a
licensed label, not an Allbirds-operated DTC business. (Competition lane; WWD 2026.)

**The pivot — "NewBird AI" / "Electronics Infrastructure Business."** Rather than
pure dissolution, management intends to **rename the public shell** and reinvent it
as an **Electronics Infrastructure Business**: buying NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs through a
new wholly-owned subsidiary **"NewBird AI, LLC"** and monetizing them via
leases / sale-leasebacks. The company's own supplemental risk factors call the new
business **"highly speculative, uncertain, unproven"** with **"no operating history."**
The DEFM14A legally calls it the "Electronics Infrastructure Business," states the
board **"has not yet made a final decision on a new name,"** and that it is merely
**"investigating opportunities."** **Original dissolution / Plan-of-Distribution
remains the explicit fallback** ("distributions to stockholders, if any") and is still
on the June-3 ballot.
(Sources: Ex. 99.2 to 4/20/2026 8-K; DEFM14A 2026-05-08.)

**Sole operating proof point of the pivot.** A NewBird subsidiary escrowed funds to
buy NVIDIA Blackwell GPU servers and leased them via an **~$2.75M, 3-year lease to a
subsidiary of QumulusAI, Inc.** (an opaque early-stage "neocloud"). That is the entire
new "business" to date. (Sources: 8-K acc 0001193125-26-164338; DEFM14A.)

**Reconciliation of "dissolution" vs "AI pivot" (lanes initially appeared to
conflict).** They are **two stages of one story**: the FY2025 10-K (filed 2026-03-31)
announced the asset sale **and a planned dissolution**; ~16 days later (2026-04-14/15)
management **reversed** to keep the shell and pivot to GPU leasing. Both the pivot and
a dissolution proposal now **coexist on the single June-3 ballot.** (Adjudicated across
filings / financials / news / leadership lanes — no factual conflict, only sequencing.)

  Q1-2026 −$12.1M (~$20M/qtr total loss). Capex cut to maintenance-only (~$3M, ~2% of

  (Sources: 8-Ks; DEFM14A.)
- **Standing dilution machinery.** A **Chardan ATM** equity program (up to 3%

  replacing a prior TD Securities ATM.

### 2.4 The $50M financing — a classic death-spiral convertible

- **A&R Securities Purchase Agreement (2026-04-19; original 2026-04-14)** for **up to
  $50M senior secured convertible notes** with one institutional investor.
- **Terms:** **12.0% coupon, 5% OID, 24-month maturity, 17% late charge**;
  **"Alternate Conversion Price" = 93% of the lowest 10-day VWAP (85% over 15 days on
  default)**; **25% redemption/default & change-of-control premiums**; **4.99%
  ownership blocker** (9.99% cited in one lane); and a **24-month co-investment right
  for ≥55% of all future Company/NewBird financings** on the same terms.
- **Security:** the GPUs + NewBird equity and, post-sale, all Company assets.
- **Committed vs optional (critical):** only **~$5.25M is committed** ($3.25M issued at
  initial close + $2.0M conditioned on the Nasdaq Proposal passing June 3); the
  **remaining ~$44.75M is solely at the investor's option** — so the headline "$50M"
  **materially overstates committed capital.**
- **Mandatory redemptions:** 100% of asset-sale proceeds and 25% of any subsequent
  placement. (Sources: 8-K acc 0001193125-26-164338; DEFM14A p.82.)
- **Dilution math (at an illustrative $2.988 conversion):** Class A shares issued rise
  from ~7.31M (notes $3.25M) to **~22.95M (full $50M), giving the investor ~72.9% of
  Class A.** Because the conversion price floats DOWN with the stock, **dilution
  worsens as the equity weakens.** (DEFM14A p.82; valuation-data.csv.)

## 4. Moat & Competition

**The forward business being valued is NewBird AI (GPUaaS / "neocloud").** Treat its
market share as **~0%** — no disclosed customers, revenue, utilization, or pricing as
of 2026-06-01. (Competition lane.)

**Market structure is brutally consolidated *and* fragmented.** SemiAnalysis
ClusterMAX 2.0 (2025-11-06) rated 84 providers and tracked **209**. **CoreWeave is the
sole "Platinum" tier** (FY2025 rev $5.1B, +168%, ~45,000 GPUs, NVIDIA "Elite," ~$14.2B
Meta commitment). Gold = Nebius/Oracle/Azure/Crusoe/Fluidstack; Silver = Google/AWS/
Together.ai/Lambda. Nebius FY2025 rev ~$530M is ~1/10th of CoreWeave. **NewBird is
below the rated universe — an unrated micro-entrant** (~4 orders of magnitude smaller
than CoreWeave by GPU count).

**Porter Five Forces (GPUaaS) — uniformly unfavorable to a sub-scale entrant:**
- **Rivalry:** extreme (209 providers; thesis that a handful control 80%+ by 2027).
- **Barriers to a durable position:** very high — **power allocation is the binding
  constraint**, ~$3.1T into chips by 2030, GPU capex ~3.7× hosting cost, NVIDIA
  allocation gating, SLURM/K8s orchestration, InfiniBand, SOC2/ISO.
- **Supplier power:** very high (NVIDIA + scarce power + a single convertible
  investor).
- **Buyer power:** high (compute is commoditizing).
- **Substitutes:** high (hyperscalers, ~200 neoclouds, self-build, TPUs/AMD).
(Sources: Vultr 2026-01-28; SemiAnalysis 2025-11-06.)

**Unit economics are thin and capital-punishing.** Post-depreciation gross margins of
**14–16%**; capital recovery required within **48–60 months** before funding the next
GPU generation; GPU pricing **~−50% over five years**. Vultr (2026-01-28) explicitly
concludes small-scale survival is **"unlikely."** Constellation Research (2026-04-15):
NewBird **"is going to need a lot more than $50 million to become a neocloud."**

**No durable moat for NewBird on any axis** [JUDGMENT, well-evidenced]: switching costs
LOW; scale economics ADVERSE; network effects NONE; brand equity in AI infra ZERO (a
footwear name being sold off); no favorable regulatory barrier. The cost-advantaged
winners (crypto-miner pivots — TeraWulf, IREN, Applied Digital) win on **pre-existing
gigawatt-scale power + capital** — exactly what NewBird lacks.

**Stacked single-points-of-failure:** capacity depends on **one partner** (QumulusAI —
itself a small/mid, debt-funded neocloud targeting >21,000 Blackwell GPUs by end-2026,
backed by a $500M USD.AI facility + $45M convertible) and capital depends on **one
convertible investor** holding a **55% co-invest right**. Either relationship souring
collapses the model. **Demand-side concentration is endemic even for the leader**
(CoreWeave ~67% of FY2025 revenue from Microsoft).

**Legacy footwear competitive decline (context only).** ~$4.1B peak valuation
(Nov-2021 IPO) → $39M IP sale (~99% destruction). Revenue fell every year post-2022;
share lost to **On** (sales +32–36% YoY) and **Hoka** (FY2025 $2.23B, +23.6%, +2 pts
US road-running share); the sustainability differentiator was commoditized by
Nike/Adidas/Puma. (WWD 2026; Glossy 2025; SGB Media 2025.) The brand survives as a
licensed AXNY label, not an operating DTC competitor.

## 5. Sector & Macro

**Two sectors in play.** Go-forward thesis = **AI compute / neocloud / GPUaaS**;
legacy DTC footwear matters only as discontinued context.

**TAM / growth (AI compute) — fast but scope-dependent.** Neocloud aggregate ~$25B
FY25 → ~$400B by 2031 (~**58% CAGR**, Synergy Research 2026-04-02) or $236.5B by 2031
(**46% CAGR**, Mordor); narrow GPUaaS slower at **~26–29% CAGR** (MarketsandMarkets,
Grand View). [JUDGMENT: growth is not the question — *who earns the economics* is.]

**Demand engine.** Big-4 hyperscaler 2026 capex guided **~$660B (+77% YoY)**; Goldman
sees **$1.15T over 2025–27**. Demand shifting from bursty training toward recurring
**inference (~80% of neocloud by 2030, ABI)** — a durability tailwind.

**Return on capital (the crux) is UNKNOWN/contested.** Capex is ~80% GPUs; GAAP returns
are thin despite high "adjusted" margins — **CoreWeave GAAP operating margin was
−3% / 2% / 4% across Q1–Q3 2025 vs ~62% adj. EBITDA** (the gap is GPU depreciation +
interest). **No clean audited multi-year neocloud ROIC exists** (CoreWeave IPO'd 2025).
[JUDGMENT: the opposite of the durable high-ROIC franchise a Munger lens prefers.]

**Three structural headwinds:**
1. **Commoditization.** H100 on-demand rents fell **~64%** from a ~$8/hr peak to
   ~$2.85–3.50 (long-tail <$1); AWS cut H100 ~30% in Jun-2025. Asset-viability floor
   ~$1.65/hr; equity-return hurdle ~$2.85/hr (Introl). **Conflicting signal:** NVIDIA
   reportedly raised 2026 H100 rents ~20% and near-term capacity is pre-booked through
   ~Aug–Sep 2026. [JUDGMENT: trust secular deflation for prior-gen vintages; frontier
   Blackwell/Rubin stays scarce near-term.]
2. **Bubble / circular financing + leverage.** Mega-caps act as supplier+customer+
   investor+validator simultaneously (NVIDIA↔OpenAI↔Oracle↔CoreWeave). Concrete crack:
   **OpenAI missed internal revenue/user targets, triggering an AI-infra selloff
   2026-04-28.** Builds are debt-funded (~$1.5T projected AI-infra debt issuance;
   CoreWeave interest expense tripled to $311M, ~$34B off-balance-sheet leases).
3. **Power is the binding constraint.** ~Half of 2026-planned US AI datacenters are
   canceled/delayed (~5GW building vs ~16GW announced); new NoVa interconnection
   entrants (May-2026) can't draw power until ~2030–2033; DOE/LBNL project datacenters
   up to 12% of US electricity by 2030.

**Cycle position [JUDGMENT, 2026-06-01]:** late-stage euphoria with first visible
cracks — peak optimism ($660B capex, 300+ entrants in 2025) colliding with early
oversupply (H100 −64%, AWS cut, OpenAI miss, ~half of 2026 builds canceled). **Textbook
capital-cycle danger zone for a new sub-scale entrant.**

**Regulatory regime is policy-volatile.** Commerce loosened China AI-chip exports
2026-01-13 then re-tightened edges 2026-05-31 (covers Blackwell/Rubin; applies to
China-HQ entities anywhere). **Remote Access Security Act** (House 369-22, Jan-2026)
closes the GPU "cloud loophole" — a real KYC/compliance burden a tiny operator inherits
with little staff.

**The 2–3 variables that drive sector economics:** (1) GPU rental rate × utilization
net of vintage decay; (2) cost & availability of capital + NVIDIA allocation;
(3) power access & cost (4–7+ yr interconnection queues now gate who can build).

**Legacy sector (divested context).** Athletic footwear ~$145–183B (2025), mature
~5–7% CAGR, Nike/Adidas-dominated; DTC under structural pressure (CAC, returns, store
capital intensity, tariffs). [JUDGMENT: Allbirds is a company-specific execution failure
within a survivable sector — peer **Warby Parker grew +12–15%** over the same window.]

## 6. Leadership & Capital Allocation

**Control — founder/VC, dual-class, minority Class A powerless.** Class B = 10
votes/share, Class A = 1 vote. Per 2025 DEF 14A (as of 2025-03-31) all directors &
officers held **85.5% of Class B = 71.3% of total voting power on just 3.0% of Class
A**. Co-founders **Brown (~22.4% of votes) + Zwillinger (~20.2%)** ≈ 42.6%;
**Maveron / Dan Levitan 27.2%** (Levitan on board). Fidelity holds 13.2% of Class A but
only ~2.4% of votes. **CEO Vernachio's economic stake is <1%.**

**Capital allocation = serial value destruction** [JUDGMENT, well-evidenced].
IPO Nov-2021 at $15 (peak valuation >$4B, raised ~$303M gross / ~$270M net) → ~$38M
market cap with the **entire operating business being sold for $39M ⇒ ~99% equity value
destroyed.** Capital consumed by operating losses and an over-built store rollout; **no
value-creating buybacks, never a dividend**; a 1-for-20 reverse split (Sep-2024) was
needed for Nasdaq compliance; recent moves are dilutive/defensive (ATM + toxic
convertible).

**Revolving-door C-suite; no relevant expertise for the pivot.** Two CEO changes in
~12 months (co-founder Brown out as co-CEO May-2023; co-founder/CEO **Zwillinger out
Mar-2024**); CFO turnover (Mitchell since Apr-2023); board churn (Fields resigned
Sep-2024; Blumenthal did not re-stand Apr-2025). **CEO Joe Vernachio** (footwear/apparel:
Mountain Hardwear, The North Face, Patagonia, Nike; base $500K) and **CFO Ann Mitchell**
(Gymshark, adidas; base $385K) have **NO technology/cloud/data-center/GPU/enterprise-IT
background** — yet are steering an AI-compute pivot. **No AI/cloud executives or
directors have been named.** Co-founder **Joey Zwillinger RESIGNED from the Board
