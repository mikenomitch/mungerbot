**Data table artifact:** [`artifacts/bird-crowd-signal.md`](../../artifacts/bird-crowd-signal.md)

> **Framing.** Everything here is *sentiment / positioning*, an explicit cross-check
> — **not** truth and **not** a fundamental view. BIRD is a ~$37M-cap microcap mid-
> transformation (footwear asset sale + "NewBird AI" GPU pivot), so the usual
> institutional crowd-signal infrastructure (deep sell-side, liquid options, a
> prediction market) is largely **absent or distorted**. The dominant signal is the
> **June 3, 2026 shareholder vote** (2 days after the as-of date) and the stock's own
> meme-driven volatility.

---

## TL;DR (one-screen)

1. **No prediction markets exist** on BIRD/NewBird (Polymarket, Kalshi, Metaculus all checked — none). Expected for a sub-$50M name.
2. **Best "deal-odds" proxy = locked-up vote:** ~**71% of voting power** committed via Support Agreements (Maveron, founders, board) ahead of the **June 3, 2026** special meeting ⇒ asset sale **highly likely to pass**. Outcome not yet known as-of 2026-06-01.
3. **Sell-side is effectively gone:** ~1 analyst, stale "Hold," $8 target referencing *2023* footwear guidance. **No live forward consensus** — ignore legacy targets.
4. **Options give no usable forward signal:** 30-day *implied* vol not populated (illiquid); 30-day *realized* vol ~**87%**, beta **2.57** — extreme-vol regime.
5. **Short interest ~1.0–1.1M sh, ~16–18% of float** (S3 estimated 16–21% pre-pivot). April squeeze featured "no borrow / minimal float." Current borrow rate = **data gap** (paywalled).
6. **Crowd vs. fundamentals:** a ~**580–600% one-day spike** (Apr 15) on a business with no demonstrated expertise = textbook froth; press anchored it to the **Long Island Iced Tea → "Long Blockchain"** delisting precedent.

## 2. The June 3, 2026 shareholder vote — the dominant catalyst (and best event-odds proxy)

This is the single most important forward event and it sits **2 days after the
as-of date**, so the result is **unknown as of 2026-06-01**.

- **Meeting:** virtual special meeting, **2026-06-03, 12:00 p.m. PT**, audio webcast.
  **Rescheduled from 2026-05-18.** Source: SEC DEFM14A (filed 2026-05-08) and
  rescheduling disclosure; corroborated TipRanks/StockTitan coverage of the proxy.
- **Proposals on the ballot:** (1) Asset Sale to American Exchange Group (~$39M);
  (2) Charter Amendment; (3) **Nasdaq Proposal** (authorizing share issuance on
  convertible conversion); (4) **Dissolution Proposal**; (5) Adjournment.
  Source: DEFM14A 2026-05-08.
- **Board:** unanimously recommends "FOR" all five. Source: DEFM14A 2026-05-08.
- **Locked-up support — the quasi-prediction-market datapoint:** the company signed
  **Support Agreements covering ~71% of total voting power** (as of 2026-02-28),
  including **Maveron** (>5% Class B holder), **Joey Zwillinger**, **Tim Brown**, and
  **Dick Boyce** (all directors). With Class B super-voting amplification, this makes
  the **asset sale approval near-certain**. Source: DEFM14A / 8-K, 2026
  (StockTitan: "Allbirds secures 71% voting backing for asset sale").
- **Why the vote gates the whole pivot:** the convertible facility draws beyond the
  initial $3.25M (the extra $2.0M of the first $5.25M, and the path to the full
  $50M) are **conditioned on the Nasdaq Proposal passing** at this meeting. So a
  "yes" both (a) divests footwear and (b) unlocks the AI-pivot financing. Source:
  SEC 8-K, Securities Purchase Agreement dated 2026-04-14 (A&R 2026-04-19).
- **The live fork the market hasn't resolved:** the *same* meeting carries a
  **Dissolution Proposal**, and the proxy contemplates full dissolution within ~12
  months. So even a "yes" leaves a genuine fork between **AI-pivot execution** and
  **wind-down + cash distribution to shareholders** (net proceeds distribution was
  originally guided to Q3 2026). The crowd is pricing *approval*; it is **not**
  cleanly pricing *which path* follows. Sources: DEFM14A 2026-05-08; StockTitan APA
  coverage (2026-03-30) describing the original dissolution/wind-down plan.

> **Conflict noted & adjudicated:** Early deal coverage (StockTitan, 2026-03-30)
> framed the transaction as a **dissolution with proceeds returned to shareholders
> (no go-forward entity)**. The **Apr 15** announcement reframed it as a **go-forward
> AI pivot ("NewBird AI")** funded by the convertible. Both are real and now coexist
> on one ballot — the company kept *optionality* between distribute-and-dissolve and
> pivot-and-build. This is why the crowd signal is muddy: bulls price the AI call
> option; the structure still permits liquidation.

## 5. Short interest, days-to-cover, and borrow stress

- **Current short interest (NASDAQ bi-monthly):** **~1,012,875 shares**, **~16.6–17.4%
  of float**, on the 2026-05-15 settlement (carried to 2026-05-29 on Fintel).
  Down from a 2026-04-30 peak of **1,108,752** and roughly flat vs. the 2026-03-31
  baseline of 1,041,526. Sources: MarketBeat (2026-05-15), Fintel (2026-05-29).
- **Pre-pivot positioning:** **S3 Partners estimated 16–21% of shares shorted** before
  the Apr-15 spike. Source: James Seyffart (S3 data) via X, Apr 2026.
- **Days-to-cover is distorted and should be discounted.** Reported DTC swung
  4.17 (Mar 31) → 0.02 (Apr 15) → 0.16 (Apr 30) → 1.9 (May 15) → 2.2 (May 29). The
  sub-1 readings are artifacts of the April volume explosion (avg daily volume
  ~250k–500k+), not evidence shorts can exit freely. Use share count and %-float as
  the durable signals. Sources: MarketBeat, Fintel.
- **Borrow / hard-to-borrow:** during the April squeeze the name traded with
  "minimal free float and no way to borrow shares" (trade press). The **specific
  cost-to-borrow rate and shares-available are PAYWALLED** (Fintel/S3) — **DATA GAP**
  as of 2026-06-01. Qualitatively, this was a hard-to-borrow, squeeze-prone setup.
  Sources: MEXC/Cryptopolitan (Apr 2026); Fintel (paywall noted 2026-05-29).
- **Off-exchange short-volume ratio** ~62.4% (Fintel, 2026-05-29) — consistent with a
  retail-heavy, low-float micro-cap; not independently verified.
- **Read:** a persistent ~16–18% short position into a 71%-locked "yes" vote and a
  binary AI-pivot-vs-dissolution fork = a crowded, two-sided, squeeze-prone book.
  Shorts likely express either the "dissolution-leaves-little-residual-equity-value"
  view or the "froth-mean-reverts" view; the low float makes either side fragile.

## 6. The stock itself as the loudest crowd signal (froth vs. fundamentals)

- **Last price $4.18; mkt cap ~$36.8M; EV ~$55.7M (> cap ⇒ net debt incl. the
  convertible); cash ~$14.4M; 50-/200-day MA $5.15/$5.07; -40.7% over 52 weeks.**
  Source: stockanalysis.com, 2026-06-01.
- **The Apr-15 pivot melt-up:** +~**580–600%** intraday — $6.82 open → $24.31 high →
  $16.99 close — adding ~$120–150M of market cap on the *announcement* of entering a
  business it has never operated. Reversed -25% to -36% the next day (closed ~$12.03).
  Sources: CNN, CNBC, CoinDesk, Bloomberg (2026-04-15/16); StocksToTrade (04-16).
- **Skeptic framing (the crowd-vs-fundamentals gap, in others' words):**
  - **Steve Sosnick (Interactive Brokers, chief strategist):** "the motivation behind
    the corporate pivot is sensible, the market reaction less so… a 6x or 7x move for
    a company that is literally ditching its prior business model for one in which it
    has no demonstrated expertise says quite a bit about market froth." Source:
    Fool / trade press, Apr 2026.
  - **Sherwood News (2026-04-15):** explicitly compared the pivot to **Long Island
    Iced Tea's 2017 blockchain rebrand ("Long Blockchain")** — which surged ~180%+
    then was **delisted** — and questioned whether "selling GPUs to tech executives
    will prove a longer-lasting trend than selling semi-sustainable shoes."
  - **Motley Fool (2026-04-21):** "unproven GPU cloud venture"; "investors face major
    uncertainty until SEC filings clarify any sale, new strategy, or leadership."
- **Read:** the crowd signal is **bimodal**. A speculative, retail/meme-driven bull
  cohort (the AI lottery ticket) sits opposite a short cohort and a skeptical
  commentary consensus. The fundamentals (no operating AI business, $14.4M cash,
  dilutive convertible, dissolution still on the ballot) are at odds with the
  episodic spikes. This is precisely the "crowd vs. fundamentals disagree" case the
  lane is meant to flag.
