## 0. The one-paragraph story

Allbirds is **not, as of 2026-06-01, the sustainable-footwear company its ticker history implies.**
Over the trailing ~18 months it executed a 1-for-20 reverse split (Sep 2024) to keep its Nasdaq
listing, then a turnaround under CEO Joe Vernachio (US store closures, shift to wholesale +
international distributors), then — after a FY2025 with a **going-concern qualification** (revenue
−19.7% to $152.5M; net loss −$77.3M; operating cash flow −$55.1M) — agreed on **2026-03-29 to sell
the entire Allbirds brand and IP to American Exchange Group (AXNY) for ~$39M** and wind down the
footwear company. Two weeks later (**2026-04-14/15**) it announced a **$50M senior secured
convertible facility** and a **pivot of the surviving listed shell into an AI/GPU-compute business
("NewBird AI")**, sending the stock **+582% in a day** (market cap ~$21M → ~$148M). The market has
since faded the move (last ~$4.29). **The deal is not yet done:** the decisive **stockholder vote is
a future event — a virtual Special Meeting on 2026-06-03** (two days after the as-of date). Co-founder
Joey Zwillinger resigned the Board on 2026-05-18.

This lane's central signal-vs-noise judgment: **the AI pivot is a financing/narrative event grafted
onto an asset sale and dissolution; the durable facts are (a) the legacy business is being sold for
cash, (b) shareholders are slated to receive ~$1.34/share, and (c) the "NewBird AI" stub is a
near-shell with only ~$5.25M of committed capital and one ~$2.75M GPU lease so far.**

### 1.3 The asset sale (M&A) — the durable transaction
- **2026-03-29:** Allbirds (a Delaware **public benefit corporation**) signs an **Asset Purchase
  Agreement** with **Allbirds IP LLC**, an affiliate of **American Exchange Group ("AXNY")** — a
  fashion-accessories design/licensing/brand-management firm. **AXNY acquires the brand, IP, and
  certain other assets and liabilities for an estimated transaction value of ~$39M**, subject to
  purchase-price adjustments. Negotiated by a **special committee of independent directors**;
  unanimous Board approval; subject to stockholder approval.
  [8-K EX-99.1, 2026-03-31](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000162828026022181/allbirdsprojectarisepressr.htm)
- **Advisors:** **TD Cowen** (financial advisor to Allbirds); **Holland & Hart LLP** (legal counsel);
  **Duff & Phelps Opinion Practice of Kroll, LLC** (fairness opinion — engaged 2026-03-19, opinion
  delivered 2026-03-27, referencing **$1.34/share**). [DEFM14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526213226/d39753ddefm14a.htm)
- **Deal protections:** a **$1.25M termination fee** payable to Buyer if Allbirds accepts a **Superior
  Proposal** (so there *is* a fiduciary out / superior-proposal mechanism); AXNY posted a **$2M
  escrow deposit**. [DEFM14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526213226/d39753ddefm14a.htm)
- **Price-discovery datapoint:** AXNY's initial **verbal proposal (2026-02-24) was $2.00–$2.25/share**;
  the negotiated outcome implies an Asset Sale Dividend of **~$1.34/share** — i.e., final value landed
  **well below the opening bid**. [DEFM14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526213226/d39753ddefm14a.htm)
- **Context (scale of the fall):** Allbirds IPO'd Nov 2021 raising ~$348M at a >$4B first-day
  valuation; the $39M sale is ~1% of that peak. [TechFundingNews](https://techfundingnews.com/allbirds-39m-asset-sale-american-exchange-group-4b-ipo-valuation/); [WWD](https://wwd.com/footwear-news/shoe-industry-news/american-exchange-group-buys-allbirds-assets-1238693817/)

### 1.4 The AI / GPU-compute pivot ("NewBird AI") — the narrative/financing event
The surviving listed entity does **not** liquidate; it dissolves the *footwear* business, distributes
the cash, and **re-purposes the public shell into an AI-compute company**.

- **2026-04-14:** **Securities Purchase Agreement** with an **unnamed institutional investor** for
  **senior secured convertible notes up to $50.0M** ("the Facility"), convertible into Class A stock,
  to fund GPU "Electronics Assets" via new wholly-owned sub **NewBird AI, LLC**. Amended & restated
  2026-04-19. [8-K, acc …164338](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526164338/d34261d8k.htm)
  - **Hard terms (material — this is a high-cost, dilutive, partly-uncommitted facility):**
    - **12.0% per annum** coupon; **5% original issue discount**; **2-year maturity**.
    - **Conversion Price = 120%** of a reference closing bid price; **Alternate Conversion Price =
      93% of the lowest 10-day VWAP** (a classic floating/"death-spiral" feature), with up to a **17%
      late charge** and a **25% premium on an event of default**.
    - **Only ~$5.25M is the initial committed tranche** ($3.25M at initial closing; +$2.0M on Nasdaq
      approval). The **remaining up to $44.75M is at the *Investor's* option** — i.e., **not committed
      capital**. Issued in multiple closings.
    - Investor gets a **right to co-invest for ≥55% of any future Company financing for 24 months.**
    - Senior secured by the Electronics Assets and the NewBird equity (and, post-close, all assets).
  - **Placement agent: Chardan.** [DEFA14A EX-99.1, 2026-04-15](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526164338/d34261dex991.htm)
- **First (and so far only) operating deal — 2026-04-14:** a NewBird subsidiary **escrowed funds to
  buy NVIDIA Blackwell GPU servers** and simultaneously signed an **~$2.75M, three-year lease** of
  those servers to **a subsidiary of QumulusAI, Inc.** (end-of-term purchase option). Described as
  "the Company's first transaction within its new business line." [8-K, acc …164338](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526164338/d34261d8k.htm)
- **Public framing (2026-04-15 press release):** pivot to "AI compute infrastructure," long-term
  vision of a **GPU-as-a-Service (GPUaaS) / AI-native "neocloud"**, planned **rename to "NewBird AI"**
  (proxy: **NewBird AI, Inc.**). The release leans on a macro thesis: AI compute demand outstripping
  supply, rising GPU lead times, record-low North American data-center vacancy.
  [DEFA14A EX-99.1](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526164338/d34261dex991.htm)
- **2026-04-28:** New **Class A ATM** Sales Agreement with **Chardan** (up to **3%** commission) under
  the existing S-3 shelf (File **333-288434**, effective 2026-07-10); **terminated the prior TD
  Securities ATM**. Another standing dilution vehicle. [8-K, acc …000006](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001653909/000165390926000006/a101allbirds-april2026atms.htm)

  atypical short-seller target; the more relevant market behavior is **retail-driven momentum** around

- **Securities class action (IPO-era):** a consolidated securities suit alleged materially

  [10-Q FY2026, acc …035302](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001653909/000162828026035302/bird-20260331.htm)
- **Company's own characterization (notable, on the record):** in the 8-K risk-factor exhibit, Allbirds
  describes its **legacy footwear business as "unsustainable and loss-making"** and warns the AI pivot
  ("Electronics Infrastructure Business") "may create additional uncertainty among employees regarding
  the Company's strategy, prospects, leadership, required skill sets and long-term organizational
  direction." [8-K EX-99.2, acc …164338](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1653909/000119312526164338/d34261dex992.htm)
- **Going-concern:** stated in the FY2025 10-K (substantial doubt); the DEFM14A notes the Company
  "anticipates continuing as a going concern" *after* the sale/dividend but flags that the convertible
  noteholders' rights "may significantly limit our strategic and operational flexibility and may create
  governance" issues, and that the structure "could increase the risk that we may be unable to continue

- **Pivot-credibility commentary (secondary):** the move has been widely framed by the trade/tech press
  as a speculative "shoes-to-servers" / "wildest pivot" story and compared to other small caps chasing
  AI; **QumulusAI (the GPU lessee) is an opaque, early-stage neocloud** with little public footprint —
  a **counterparty-quality question** for the competition/sector lanes.
