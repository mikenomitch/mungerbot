# Allbirds, Inc. (BIRD) — Valuation Model

**As-of:** 2026-06-01 · **Currency:** USD · **Prepared by:** Valuation lane (financial desk)
**Method:** Special-situation **sum-of-parts / liquidation + special-dividend** valuation, cross-checked against the company's own fairness-opinion range and an arms-length acquisition bid. A multiples-based or going-concern DCF is **not meaningful** here (see §0).

> **Decision-support only — not investment advice. Not an order to trade.**

---

## 0. Why standard multiples and a forward DCF do not apply

Allbirds is mid-transformation as of the as-of date:

1. **Footwear business is being sold and exited.** A definitive Asset Purchase Agreement (March 29, 2026) sells the Allbirds brand, IP, inventory and contracts to American Exchange Group for a **$39.0M** stated price. Net proceeds est. **$35.0M–$36.2M** after expenses/taxes. Shareholder vote at the Special Meeting **June 3, 2026**. (SEC DEFM14A, 2026-05-08.)
2. **The remaining entity becomes "NewBird AI,"** an AI-compute / GPU-as-a-Service shell with **no operating history** — a single ~$2.75M, 3-yr GPU lease to a QumulusAI subsidiary using NVIDIA Blackwell servers is its only transaction. (SEC 8-K, 2026-04-14; DEFM14A.)
3. **Going concern:** audited FY2025 statements carry **substantial-doubt** language. Q1-2026 cash $14.4M; Q1 net loss $20.7M; operating cash burn $12.1M/quarter. (10-Q FY2026 Q1; SEC XBRL.)

Therefore P/E, EV/EBIT and EV/FCF are **negative/meaningless** (no positive earnings or FCF, business discontinued). The legacy multiples are shown in §3 only to document the collapse. The defensible value is **cash/asset value distributable to holders (the special dividend) + an option value on an unproven AI pivot**, which is exactly what the company's banker modeled.

## 4. Reverse-DCF — what does the current price imply?

A conventional reverse-DCF on earnings is impossible (no positive cash flow). Instead, **reverse-engineer the value the AI pivot must create** to justify today's price, given the asset value is already known:

- Market cap at $4.18 ≈ **$36.6M**.
- Best-case distributable asset value to equity (D&P high) ≈ **$16.9M** (~$1.83/sh).
- **Implied value the market assigns to the un-launched "NewBird AI" business ≈ $36.6M − $16.9M ≈ ~$20M** (and ~$37M vs. the D&P midpoint of ~$8.5M ⇒ ~$28M of pivot value).

**Is ~$20M of NewBird AI value plausible today?** It would require the market to believe the GPUaaS shell — currently a **single ~$2.75M lease**, no operating history, funded by a **toxic convertible** (only $5.25M committed, rest optional) — is already worth multiples of its lone asset. Embedded implied assumptions:
- That the optional $44.75M of the convertible gets drawn and deployed into productive GPU assets at attractive spreads (a neocloud earning a return on leased Blackwell GPUs);
- That dilution does not destroy per-share value (see §5 — full conversion at $2.988 gives the investor ~73% of Class A; the **alternate conversion at 93% of VWAP** issues *more* shares as the price falls);
- That the company survives the going-concern window and public-company costs on minimal retained cash.

**Verdict:** the price implies a substantial, speculative going-concern premium for an unproven pivot, **on top of** an asset value the company's own banker caps at $1.83/share. That premium is not supported by any disclosed contracted revenue, backlog, or unit economics.

## 5. Dilution overhang (convertible note) — quantified

A&R Securities Purchase Agreement (4/14–4/19/2026): up to **$50.0M** senior secured convertible notes; **only $5.25M committed** ($3.25M issued + $2.0M after Nasdaq vote); remaining **$44.75M at the Investor's sole option**. 12% coupon, 5% OID, 2-yr maturity, secured by NewBird assets + (post-close) all company assets. Investor gets **55% co-investment right** on future financings for 24 months. (SEC 8-K 2026-04-14, Item 1.01.)

**Conversion price = 120% × lower of (x) bid the day before the initial proxy filing and (y) bid the day before each tranche closes.** "Alternate Conversion Price" = lower of conversion price or **93% of lowest 10-day VWAP** (85% if event of default) — a classic **floating / death-spiral** feature that issues more shares as the price falls. 4.99% ownership blocker (raisable to 9.99%).

Dilution at the proxy's stated $2.988 conversion price (DEFM14A p.82):

| Notes converted | New Class A shares | Total Class A after | Investor % of Class A |
|---|---|---|---|
| $3.25M (issued) | 1,087,684 | 7,308,480 | 14.88% |
| $5.25M (committed) | 1,757,028 | 7,977,824 | 22.02% |
| $10M | 3,346,720 | 9,567,516 | 34.98% |
| $25M | 8,366,800 | 14,587,597 | 57.36% |
| $50M | 16,733,601 | 22,954,397 | **72.90%** |

At a **lower** share price the alternate conversion issues proportionally more shares — dilution is *worse* precisely when the equity is weakest. This caps realistic per-share upside for common holders even if the pivot partially works.

## 6. Three-scenario forward valuation (per share)

Because there is no cash-flow base to DCF, scenarios are built on **(a) the special dividend the board funds from net proceeds + (b) residual value of the NewBird AI stub net of dilution.** All highly judgment-laden; the special-dividend split is **undisclosed** (board discretion).

| | **Bear** | **Base** | **Bull** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Asset sale closes? | Yes (or fails → ruin) | Yes | Yes |
| Net proceeds to firm | $35.0M | $35.6M | $36.2M |
| Less: residual liabilities / ABL not covered by acquired liabs, wind-down, reserves, escrow holdback | high | mid | low |
| Cash retained for AI pivot | most retained, little dividend | ~half | modest retain, larger dividend |
| **Special dividend / share** | ~$0.50 | ~$1.25 | ~$1.75 |
| NewBird AI stub value / share (post-dilution) | ~$0.10 (fails, burns cash, toxic conversion) | ~$0.40 (survives, small lease book) | ~$1.25 (scales GPUaaS, draws capital accretively) |
| **Total intrinsic value / share** | **~$0.60** | **~$1.65** | **~$3.00** |
| Probability (judgment) | 35% | 45% | 20% |
| **Prob-weighted value** | | **~$1.58** | |

**Bear tail not in the table — deal failure = ruin:** if the Asset Sale is voted down or fails, the proxy states the company "does not anticipate it would be able to meet its future liquidity needs" — i.e., insolvency / near-zero equity. The toxic convertible (mandatory redemption of 100% of asset-sale proceeds, 25% premiums on default/change-of-control) is senior and could absorb proceeds in a stress case.

## 7. Sensitivity

**To IP recovery (largest swing factor in the asset value):**
| IP Asset value | Net liquidation value | Per share |
|---|---|---|
| $30M (low) | ~$0.2M | ~$0.02 |
| $35M (mid) | ~$8.5M | ~$0.92 |
| $40M (high) | ~$16.9M | ~$1.83 |

The entire equity value is effectively a **levered bet on the IP sale price**: the ABL ($18.9M), AP ($23.8M) and other claims consume nearly all gross asset value, so a $10M move in IP value swings per-share value by ~$1.80.

**To the special-dividend split (board discretion):** every $9.2M the board distributes ≈ **$1.00/share** of dividend; every dollar retained shifts value into the higher-risk NewBird stub.

**Discount rate / terminal growth:** a conventional WACC/g sensitivity is not meaningful (no cash-flow stream). The IP relief-from-royalty DCF used royalty 4–6%, tax 28%, terminal g 3% — already embedded in the $30–40M IP range above.

## 8. Conclusion — intrinsic value vs. price & margin of safety

| | Value |
|---|---|
| **Intrinsic-value range (adopted)** | **~$0.50 – $2.25 / share** (D&P liquidation $0.02–$1.83 + AEG bid $2.00–$2.25; midpoint ≈ **$1.40**) |
| Probability-weighted point estimate | **~$1.55 / share** |
| Current price (2026-06-01 close) | **$4.18 / share** |
| **Margin of safety at midpoint $1.40** | **≈ −199%** (price is ~3x the midpoint) |
| Margin of safety vs. best case ($2.25) | **≈ −86%** (price is ~1.9x even the most generous anchor) |

**The stock trades ABOVE the top of every disclosed intrinsic-value anchor — including the company's own fairness opinion high ($1.83) and the arms-length whole-company bid ($2.25).** There is **negative** margin of safety on the evidence. The gap is a speculative premium on the unproven NewBird AI pivot, against a backdrop of going-concern doubt and a dilutive/death-spiral convertible.

**Method & weakest assumption:** Method = sum-of-parts (distributable asset value + AI-stub option), anchored to the company's banker liquidation analysis and an arms-length bid. **Weakest assumptions:** (1) the **undisclosed special-dividend split** — the single biggest determinant of common-holder value, fully at board discretion; (2) the **IP sale price** ($30–40M), to which equity value is highly levered; (3) the **value of the NewBird AI stub**, which has no operating history and could be anywhere from ~$0 to a meaningful neocloud — estimated here judgmentally.
