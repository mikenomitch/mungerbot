## 1. Headline picture (the one-paragraph version)

URBN is a **debt-free, net-cash specialty-apparel retailer** ($6.17B FY2026 revenue) that has spent the
post-COVID period steadily **expanding margins** (gross 36.0% and operating 9.8% are both 10-year highs),
**compounding returns on capital** (ROIC ~14% reported / ~18% on a lease-inclusive NOPAT basis, ROE ~17.6%),
and **shrinking the share count** (−21.7% over nine years) via buybacks while **paying no dividend**. Growth
is increasingly driven by two structurally better businesses than legacy retail: **Nuuly** (apparel rental
subscription, +50% revenue, +45% subscribers) and **Free People / FP Movement**. The balance sheet carries
**zero funded debt** — the only large liability is capitalized operating leases (~$1.0B) — and ~$1.16B of
cash + marketable securities. Accounting quality looks clean (tiny receivables, modest stable SBC, D&A
tracking capex, no restatements); the only watch items are **two consecutive years of inventory growing
slightly faster than sales** and a depressed FCF/NI ratio in FY2026 (0.68) driven by store + rental-fleet capex.

## 2. Revenue & segment growth

- **Total revenue FY2026 $6,165.4M, +11.1% YoY** — a record, and an acceleration from +7.7% (FY2025) and
  +7.5% (FY2024). 10-yr CAGR (FY2017 $3,545.8M → FY2026 $6,165.4M) ≈ **6.3%**; 4-yr post-COVID CAGR
  (FY2022→FY2026) ≈ **7.9%**. [SEC XBRL `RevenueFromContractWithCustomerExcludingAssessedTax`]
- **By segment (FY2026 / YoY):** Retail $5,282.7M (+7.9%); **Subscription/Nuuly $568.4M (+50.2%)**;
  Wholesale $314.3M (+14.0%). [FY26 Q4 release]
- **By brand (FY2026 / YoY / comp):** Anthropologie $2,586.6M (+6.6% / comp +5.9%); Free People
  $1,618.4M (+10.8% / comp +4.8%); Urban Outfitters $1,351.6M (+8.3% / comp +7.3%); Nuuly $568.4M (+50.2%);
  Menus & Venues $40.3M (+6.6%). [FY26 Q4 release]
- **Mix shift is favorable:** the fastest growth is Nuuly (recurring, high-incremental-margin) and the
  Free People ecosystem; the legacy/teen-exposed UO brand is the smallest of the three apparel brands and
  posted its best comp (+7.3%), suggesting a brand recovery rather than continued drag.
- **Trough/recovery context:** FY2021 revenue fell −13.4% (COVID) to $3.45B, then snapped back +31.9% in FY2022.
  FY2018 (+2.0%, NM 3.0%) was a UO-brand-specific weak year, useful as a downside reference.

## 3. Margins & their trend

- **Gross margin: 36.0% FY2026, a 10-yr high**, up from 34.7% (FY2025) and well above the 25.0% COVID trough
  (FY2021) and the 29.8% inventory-glut year (FY2023). Drivers per MD&A: higher merchandise margins +
  leverage on store occupancy and delivery/logistics as sales scaled. [10-K MD&A; GM series from XBRL `GrossProfit`/`Revenues`]
- **Operating margin: 9.8% FY2026**, up from 8.5% — also a 10-yr high; SG&A held flat at **26.2% of sales**
  in both FY2026 and FY2025, so the entire OM gain came through gross margin. [FY26 Q4 release]
- **Net margin 7.5%**; effective tax rate **~22.1%** (pre-tax $596.9M, tax $132.0M; Q4 21.9%). [Search of 10-K MD&A]
- **Adjusted vs GAAP:** FY2026 adjusted EPS $5.44 (adj. NI $499.2M) **exceeds** GAAP EPS $5.06 (NI $464.9M),
  i.e., the adjustments *added back* ~$34M — worth the dossier flagging the nature of those add-backs
  (likely a discrete charge); in FY2025 adjusted was *below* GAAP (adj. $4.03 vs GAAP $4.26). [FY26 Q4 release] **(data gap: exact add-back composition not yet pulled)**

## 4. Returns on capital — ROIC vs WACC, ROE/DuPont

- **ROE (avg-equity basis):** FY2026 **17.6%**, FY2025 17.6%, FY2024 14.7%, vs the 9.0% FY2023 / 0.1% FY2021 troughs.
- **DuPont, FY2026 (end-equity basis):** Net margin 7.54% × asset turnover 1.23 × leverage (A/E) 1.78 ≈ ROE.
  Versus FY2022 (6.83% × 1.20 × **2.17**): the ROE is now of *higher quality* — margins up, asset turns flat,
  and **financial leverage actually fell** because equity compounded while the company stayed debt-free.
- **ROIC:** reported ~**14.0%** (stockanalysis.com). My own calc: NOPAT FY2026 ≈ $472M (op. income × (1−22%)).
  - Including capitalized operating leases in invested capital and netting out cash + securities → **ROIC ≈ 17.8%**.
  - Excluding leases (equity − cash − securities) → **ROIC ≈ 28.5%**.
  - ROIC has roughly **tripled off the FY2022 trough (~6%)**.
- **ROIC vs WACC:** a reasonable WACC for an unlevered apparel retailer is ~**8–10%**. On any of the above
  bases URBN earns **well above** its cost of capital, i.e., it is creating economic value as it grows.
  *(Judgment; WACC is an estimate, not a disclosed figure — see data gaps.)*

## 5. Free cash flow & conversion

- **FY2026: OCF $575.2M − capex $260.2M = FCF $315.0M (5.1% FCF margin).** OCF has grown every year since FY2023.
- **FCF/NI conversion 0.68 in FY2026** — *below* 1.0 and below FY2024 (1.08). Causes: (i) elevated store capex +
  Nuuly rental-fleet/fulfillment investment (capex 4.2% of sales, near the high end of its 3–6% range);
  (ii) inventory build (+$72.8M cash use). This is **growth capex, not maintenance** — a quality-of-FCF nuance,
  not a red flag, but the dossier should not treat reported NI as a clean cash proxy this year.
- **History:** FCF positive in 8 of the last 10 years; the exception was **FY2023 (−$56.8M)**, a post-COVID
  inventory-over-ordering year that has fully reversed. 10-yr cumulative FCF ≈ **$1,991M**. [XBRL OCF, capex]
- **D&A ~$128.5M** in FY2026, tracking capex over time (no signs of under-depreciation).

## 6. Balance-sheet strength, net debt, maturity wall

- **ZERO funded/long-term debt.** The XBRL tag `LongTermDebtNoncurrent` is **empty for every year** — URBN has
  not carried bonds or term loans. **There is no debt maturity wall.** [SEC XBRL — tag absent]
- **What aggregators call "debt" (~$1.23B at FY2026) is entirely capitalized operating leases**
  (`OperatingLeaseLiabilityNoncurrent` $1,000.1M noncurrent + current portion). Real, but operating, not financing.
- **Liquidity:** cash & equivalents $369.2M + marketable securities $788.6M = **~$1,157.8M total cash+investments**
  (≈ $12.6/share). **Net cash** even against gross lease liabilities if one chooses; against funded debt the
  net-cash position is unambiguous. Current ratio **1.51** (up from 1.29 in FY2024). [FY26 Q4 release; XBRL]
- **No pension obligation** of note; principal long-dated commitment is store leases. No covenant constraints
  (no debt to covenant). **(data gap: revolver size/terms not pulled — URBN historically keeps an undrawn ABL.)**

## 7. Working capital & capex intensity

- **DSO ~5–6 days** (receivables $95.7M on $6.17B revenue) — mostly card settlement + Free People wholesale.
  Minimal credit/collection risk; the +29% YoY receivables jump is off a tiny base (Nuuly + wholesale growth).
- **Inventory $700.9M, +12.8% YoY**, i.e., **11.4% of revenue** — within the post-COVID 10.7–12.5% band but
  **growing faster than sales for the 2nd straight year** (FY2025 inv +12.9% vs sales +7.7%; FY2026 +12.8% vs +11.1%).
  *Watch item* — could pressure future markdowns — but GM is *rising*, so no realized margin damage yet, and
  some build supports Nuuly's rental fleet and a larger store base. DIO is healthy for the format.
- **Capex intensity 3–6% of revenue**; FY2026 at the upper end (4.2%) on store openings (69 opened, 18 closed →
  784 company-owned) and Nuuly capacity. Maintenance capex is well below D&A; the increment is growth.

## 8. Share count, buybacks, dividends

- **Diluted shares 91.8M (FY2026), down −21.7% from 117.3M (FY2017).** FY2026 buyback 3.3M shares for ~$154M;
  **14.6M shares still authorized** at Jan 31 2026. [FY26 Q4 release; XBRL `WeightedAverageNumberOfDilutedSharesOutstanding`]
- **No dividend, ever** (`CommonStockDividendsPerShareDeclared` empty across all years). 100% of cash return = buybacks.
- **Capital-return scale:** cumulative buybacks FY2017–FY2026 ≈ **$923M** (≈38% of cumulative NI of $2,419M;
  ≈46% of cumulative FCF of $1,991M). Buybacks are opportunistic/lumpy (e.g., $0 in FY2024, ~$217M in FY2020),
  consistent with a value-conscious approach rather than a fixed program — a capital-allocation signal the
  leadership lane should weigh.
- **SBC ~$30M/yr** (~0.5% of revenue), flat — buybacks are *net* reductions, not just offsetting dilution.
