## 1. Business & segments (10-K Item 1, FY2026)

URBN describes itself as "a leading lifestyle products and services company which operates a portfolio of global consumer brands." Founded 1970 (first UO store near UPenn); IPO 1993 on Nasdaq. **~12% 5-yr sales CAGR**; FY2026 sales ~$6.2B. Home office: 5000 South Broad Street, Philadelphia, PA. *(10-K Item 1 "Business — General")*

**Three reportable segments** *(10-K Item 1 & Note 17 Segment Reporting)*:

1. **Retail** (~85.7% of FY2026 net sales) — store + digital ("omni-channel," managed as one channel, not split). Brands & store counts as of 2026-01-31:
   - **Anthropologie** (incl. Terrain, Maeve): **254 stores** (234 N. America, 20 Europe; franchise in Middle East). Target women 28–45. ~7,200 sq ft avg. Plan FY2027: open ~14, close ~3. Anthropologie = ~49.0% of Retail net sales (47.2% N.A. + 1.8% Europe).
   - **Free People** (incl. FP Movement): **180 Free People stores** (167 N.A., 13 Europe) + **88 FP Movement stores** (all N.A.). Target women 25–30. FP ~2,300 sq ft, FP Movement ~1,500 sq ft. Plan FY2027: FP open ~13/close ~2; FP Movement open ~21. Free People = ~25.0% of Retail net sales.
   - **Urban Outfitters**: **253 stores** (177 N.A., 76 Europe; franchise in Middle East). Target young adults 18–28. ~8,400 sq ft avg. Plan FY2027: open ~8, close ~8. UO = ~25.3% of Retail net sales (15.3% N.A. + 10.0% Europe — UO is the most Europe-weighted brand).
   - **Menus & Venues**: **9 food/event locations** (all N.A.), <1% of Retail net sales.

2. **Subscription — Nuuly** (~9.2% of FY2026 net sales) — monthly women's apparel **subscription rental** (rent the company's own brands, third-party brands, vintage; can buy items). Launched 2019. Rental-product-intensive (rental product, net $246.4M at 1/31/26, in "Other assets").

3. **Wholesale** (~5.1% of FY2026 net sales) — Free People, FP Movement, and UO (BDG, "iets frans") sold to department/specialty stores (named: **Nordstrom, Dick's Sporting Goods**) via a **shop-within-shop** model. Showrooms: London, LA, NYC, Dallas. Free People is the bulk of wholesale.

**Human capital** *(10-K Item 1)*: ~**31,000 employees** at 2026-01-31, **~38% full-time**; mix ~85% Retail / 8% Subscription / 1% Wholesale / 6% corporate. Generally **non-union** (except some international). "Relations with employees are excellent" (company language).

**Sourcing / suppliers** *(10-K Item 1)*: purchased merchandise from **~4,000 vendors worldwide; no single vendor/manufacturer >10% of purchases**; **no single country represents the majority of production** (dual-sourced). This is a meaningful diversification fact and tariff mitigant.

**Seasonality** *(10-K Item 1)*: H2-weighted; more net sales/income in the back half (year-end holiday); drives working-capital/inventory build.

**Stock/dividends** *(10-K Item 5)*: 74 holders of record (2026-03-25). **Never paid a cash dividend** since IPO; does not anticipate doing so; current credit facility limits dividends.

## 2. Risk factors (10-K Item 1A) — FY2026 vs FY2025 diff

URBN groups 25 risk-factor captions under four headers: **Macroeconomic and Industry Risks · Strategic Risks · Operational Risks · Legal, Tax, Regulatory and Compliance Risks.**

**Finding: the risk-factor SET is essentially UNCHANGED year-over-year.** I extracted the bold+italic captions from both the FY2026 and FY2025 10-Ks: **25 captions in each, with a 1:1 match.** No risk factor was added or removed. The only changes are trivial wording edits:
- Risk "War, terrorism… public health crises": FY2025 said "pandemics such as COVID-19" → FY2026 reads "epidemics and pandemics" (de-emphasizing COVID specifically).
- AI risk: FY2025 "including in artificial intelligence" → FY2026 "including artificial intelligence" (cosmetic).

## 3. MD&A (10-K Item 7) — FY2026 results & narrative

**Macroeconomic / tariff disclosure (new, expanded for FY2026):** During 2025 the U.S. enacted "significant changes to its tariff regime" raising rates on many imports; foreign jurisdictions retaliated. Critically: **"In February 2026, in response to the U.S. Supreme Court invalidating many of the existing International Economic Emergency Powers Act ('IEEPA') tariffs, the government instituted incremental global tariffs on all imports and has signaled it may seek higher tariffs."** The IEEPA **refund process "is currently not finalized,"** and URBN is "analyzing available options to preserve our refund rights." Mitigations listed: negotiating vendor terms; **dual-sourcing / shifting countries of origin** (no single country is majority of production); shifting air→ocean freight; **"gently raising prices in a strategic fashion."** Management states **"tariffs could have a negative impact on our financial results"** even with mitigation. *(10-K Item 7 "Macroeconomic Environment and Other Recent Developments")*

Also flagged: **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** (enacted 2025-07-04) — tax changes (TCJA extensions, bonus depreciation); **no material impact on FY2026 income tax provision**; still being assessed.

**FY2026 results (vs FY2025)** *(10-K Item 7; cross-checked to XBRL — see artifact)*:
- Net sales **$6.165B, +11.1%**. Retail comps **+6.0%** (UO +7.3%, Anthropologie +5.9%, Free People +4.8% — note UO comp **rebounded** after −8.7% in FY2025). Subscription driven by **+45.3% avg active subscribers**. Wholesale +15.2% (Free People wholesale).
- **Gross margin 36.0%** (from 34.7%): lower markdowns (UO, FP), store-occupancy leverage, delivery leverage (fewer packages/order), partly offset by initial-merchandise-cost deleverage. Gross profit **$2.22B**.
- SG&A +11.0%; **leveraged 2 bps** as a % of sales (store-payroll leverage; marketing spend up to drive Retail/Subscription growth).
- **Income from operations $605.6M = 9.8%** of sales (from $473.8M = 8.5%).
- **$46.0M charitable contribution to a donor-advised fund** booked in "Other expense" (one-time; explains the FY2026 Other-expense jump to $45.5M).
- **Effective tax rate 22.1%** (from 19.5%) — increase due to **non-recurrence of a significant tax-reserve release** recorded in FY2025.
- **Net income $464.9M** (record), +15.5% YoY (XBRL).
- Total inventory **$700.9M, +12.8%** (Retail +13.4%; Retail comparable inventory +5.3%; Wholesale +8.5%) — "due to increase in sales and timing of inventory receipts." Inventory growing roughly in line with sales; comparable-inventory build (5.3%) below sales growth — not an obvious red flag.

**Liquidity / capital** *(10-K Item 7)*: Cash needs met from operating cash flow + marketable-securities sales/maturities. Capex (cash for P&E): **FY2026 $260.2M / FY2025 $182.6M / FY2024 $199.6M.** Financing: **FY2026 $153.9M of buybacks** (FY2025 $52.3M; FY2024 mostly payroll-tax withholding).

**Buyback program detail (10-K Note 12, R73/R75):** 20,000,000-share program authorized 2019-06-04; **14,648,609 shares remain authorized** at 1/31/26. **Under the program, FY2026 = 3,307,781 shares retired for $153,946K at $64.67 avg/share** (FY2025 = 1,200,000 shares / $52,262K / $46.54). A larger "total cost" line of $177,030K (and ~4.64M total shares retired, ~$300M cumulative value across the statement) includes program buybacks **plus** shares retired for employee payroll-tax withholding **plus** the **IRA 1% excise tax** — the $153.9M is the clean program/cash figure that ties to the cash-flow statement. **FY2027 capex guided ~$475M** (up from prior estimates) — driven partly by **purchasing the previously-leased Nuuly fulfillment center in Raymore, MO (March 2026)**. FY2027 plan: ~57 new company-owned retail locations; expand fulfillment network; "may also repurchase common shares." All FY2027 capex to be funded by operating cash flow + existing cash/securities. New-store investments "generally have the potential to generate positive cash flow within a year."

## 4. Off-balance-sheet, leases, debt, commitments

- **Funded debt: $0** across FY2024–FY2026 (XBRL). URBN runs a debt-free balance sheet on a funded-debt basis.
- **Credit facility:** an **asset-based revolving credit facility** with **JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A. (administrative agent, joint lead arranger)** and **Wells Fargo Bank, N.A.**, originally dated **June 29, 2018**, amended repeatedly (2019, 2021, 2022, 2023, and the **Fifth Amendment dated May 19, 2026** per the 2026-05-26 8-K). Borrowings bear variable rates; **a 100bp rate change "would not have a material impact to interest expense"** for FY2026 — i.e., the revolver is essentially undrawn/de-minimis. The facility includes limits on dividends and uses **stand-by letters of credit** (customs, construction, lease guarantees, insurance) and import letters of credit. *(10-K Note 8 "Debt"; Item 7A; 2026-05-26 8-K Items 1.01/2.03)*
- **Operating leases (the real "debt"):** total operating-lease liability **$1,225.6M at 1/31/26** ($225.5M current + $1,000.1M non-current); ROU asset $1,051.1M; **lease discount rate ~6.1%**; operating lease cost $285.9M; cash lease payments $314.2M. Lease liability is **off the funded-debt line but on the balance sheet** (ASC 842) — material to any EV/coverage analysis (valuation lane). *(XBRL; 10-K Note 9 "Leases")*
- **Commitments & contingencies (Note 15):** routine. Commercial commitments include stand-by letters of credit. The contractual-obligations table **excludes $92,989 (thousands? — note: as printed "$92,989") of operating-lease payments tied to the Raymore, MO Nuuly center purchased March 2026**, and excludes **$2,028 (thousands) of tax contingencies** that cannot be reasonably scheduled (classified non-current). *(10-K Item 7 contractual-obligations footnotes; Note 15)*
- **Contingencies / legal:** "party to various legal proceedings arising from normal business activities. Management believes the ultimate resolution… will not have a material adverse effect." **No specific material litigation disclosed.** *(10-K Note 15)*
- **Off-balance-sheet arrangements:** none beyond operating leases and ordinary letters of credit identified. NQDC plan obligation is funded by purchased investments held in marketable securities ($22,595K at 1/31/26).
