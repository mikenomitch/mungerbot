# URBN Competitor Matrix & Relative Trajectory

**As-of:** 2026-06-02. Compiled by the `competition` research lane. All figures cited in `research/competition/NOTES.md`. URBN's own segments map to three distinct competitive arenas: **Retail brands** (Anthropologie, Free People, Urban Outfitters namesake), **Wholesale** (Free People / FP Movement / UO branded goods sold through department & specialty stores), and **Subscription** (Nuuly apparel rental).

## 3. Subscription — apparel rental competitive set (Nuuly's arena)

| Player | Active subscribers (most recent) | Revenue | Profitability | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nuuly (URBN)** | **~420K (Feb 2026) → "crossed 500K" (Q1 FY2027 commentary)**; ~64% US rental market share | **$568M FY2026**; Q1 FY2027 $167.3M (+34.5%) | **Profitable** (achieved in FY ended 1/31/26) | **Dominant & scaling** |
| **Rent the Runway (RENT)** | ~143,796 active / ~185K total (1/31/26) | ~$330M FY2025; Q4 rev $87–91.7M | **Unprofitable** — FCF −$46M; debt restructured (−$177M LT debt, $96.3M restructuring gain drove GAAP "net income"); equity deficit; CEO Jennifer Hyman stepped down | **Struggling / survival mode** |
| Armoire, Vivrelle, Pickle, Le Tote, Haverdash, Gwynnie Bee, BNTO (URBN's own Anthropologie-adjacent test) | Small / niche | n/a | Mostly sub-scale; Vivrelle & Pickle raised tens of $M in 2024–25 | Fragmented long tail |

> **Nuuly is the clearest moat-like position in the whole company.** It is >2.5× RTR on subscribers, holds ~64% of the US rental market, is profitable while the #2 player restructures debt, and — critically — **~70% of its subscribers are NEW to clothing rental**, i.e. it is *expanding the category* rather than fighting over a fixed pool. Its structural edge: it sources ~45% of rental inventory **at cost** from sister brands (Anthropologie, Free People, Urban Outfitters), giving it a unit-economics advantage no standalone rival can replicate, plus a 600,000-sq-ft Missouri fulfillment center sized for ~3× the current subscriber base.
