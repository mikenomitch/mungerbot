# Liking / Loving Tendency — Arm Holdings (ARM)

**Discipline:** Psychology  ·  **Intensity:** lens  ·  **As-of:** 2026-06-01

Munger's Liking/Loving Tendency: humans are wired to like and love, and we
distort facts to favor the people, products, and ideas we love. Applied to
investing it cuts two ways. **Outward:** do *customers* love this brand/product
enough that the love itself produces pricing power and low churn (the
Coca-Cola / See's / Apple pattern)? **Inward:** are *we* (and the market)
loving an admired CEO, a thrilling story, or a beautiful business so much that
we are laundering enthusiasm into "analysis"? This model is a relevance check on
the moat AND a bias check on ourselves.

## What this model predicts

**If customers genuinely *love* Arm** (the favorable read), we should observe:
1. **Pricing power that customers accept with affection, not gritted teeth** —
   royalty rates rising while customers *deepen* their commitment, evangelize the
   brand, and decline to seek substitutes.
2. **Low churn / no escape-hatch behavior** — Arm's biggest, most sophisticated
   customers should NOT be funding, acquiring, or shipping alternatives.
3. **Brand affinity that transfers** — developers/OEMs choosing Arm for
   identity/trust reasons beyond raw price-performance, the way people pay up for
   Apple or Coke for what the brand *means*.

**If WE are being seduced** (the bias the model warns of), we should observe:
4. A **charismatic, widely-admired CEO** and a **thrilling, simple narrative**
   ("Arm is the toll booth on all of AI") that crowds out arithmetic.
5. Valuation set by **love of the story** rather than cash flows — price running
   ahead of the people whose job is to be sober (analysts, the company's own
   guidance).

The honest finding: **#1–#3 are only partly true and softening; #4–#5 are
strongly, almost textbook, present.** The model's most important contribution
here is as a mirror, not a moat-confirmer.

## Evidence

**Supporting — real customer attachment / stickiness (cited):**
- **Functional "love" is real and measurable.** Developers and cloud customers
  adopt Arm Neoverse for hard ROI: one migration to Arm Neoverse-based AWS
  Graviton delivered "up to 70% lower infrastructure costs," sub-20ms queries and
  billion-scale indexing (Zilliz Cloud); another cut infra cost ~25% and TF
  inference latency ~15% [Arm Newsroom, "Building the Future of Cloud Software on
  Arm Neoverse," newsroom.arm.com/blog/arm-software-ecosystem-dashboard, 2025–26].
- **Ecosystem gravity = high switching cost.** >20M developers, >75 Arm-authored
  learning paths, >325B cumulative chips, the de-facto mobile/embedded toolchain —
  a software network effect the dossier rates "WIDE but softening" [DOSSIER §1, §4;
  Arm Newsroom].
- **Near-universal installed base where it's strong:** ~99–100% of smartphone app
  processors, ~94% of automakers pay Arm [DOSSIER §1, §4.1]. That is loyalty *by
  inertia and cost-to-leave*, which is economically as good as affection — for now.
- **The royalty ladder rises and customers keep paying:** Armv8 ~2.5–3% → Armv9
  ~5% → CSS ~10%+ of chip ASP, with v9 attach targeted at 60–70% [DOSSIER §4.2].
  Customers are accepting *more* per chip — surface evidence of pricing power.

**Disconfirming — the "love" is conditional, transactional, and actively hedged
(cited):**
- **Arm's marquee customers are funding the exits.** Qualcomm acquired RISC-V
  designer **Ventana Micro (Dec-2025)** explicitly to "reduce Arm reliance"
  [Tom's Hardware, "Qualcomm's Ventana acquisition points to a long-term RISC-V
  strategy," tomshardware.com, 2026]. Google, Nvidia, Samsung, Apple are all
  working on RISC-V; Google's eng director positioned RISC-V as "a key pillar for
  future hyperscale computing" at the RISC-V Summit [The Register 2025-01-02;
  Digitimes 2026-01-05; SemiWiki forum 2026]. **You do not build an escape hatch
  from a product you love** — you build it from a landlord you tolerate.
- **A top customer *successfully resisted* and largely won.** Arm LOST the
  Qualcomm/Nuvia license fight (court granted Qualcomm JMOL 9/30/2025; Arm on
  appeal) — proving the biggest customers will fight tooth-and-nail when Arm tries
  to tighten terms [DOSSIER §4.3, §8.4]. That is the *opposite* of affectionate
  pricing power; it is adversarial bargaining.
- **Arm is now competing with the very customers it needs to love it.** The
  Mar-2026 AGI-CPU pivot makes Arm a direct rival to Qualcomm/AWS/Google/Nvidia
  merchant ambitions [DOSSIER §1, §4.3] — corroding goodwill exactly when goodwill
  is the asset. Brand-love and channel conflict do not coexist.
- **No evidence of brand affinity that transfers.** Every external source frames
  adoption in cost/perf-per-watt terms, never in identity/trust terms. Arm is a
  B2B ingredient brand to engineers optimizing a spreadsheet, not a consumer love
  object. The "Arm" logo on a phone box commands no end-user premium the way
  "Intel Inside" once did or "Apple" does. (Judgment, consistent with all sources.)

**Disconfirming — WE / the market are exhibiting Liking/Loving distortion (cited):**
- **Textbook admired-CEO dynamic.** Rene Haas: Glassdoor CEO approval **93%**,
  4.5/5 overall, 89% recommend [DOSSIER §6]; ex-Nvidia operator who doubled
  revenue and ran the IPO; hosts his own thought-leadership podcast ("Tech
  Unheard"); gives the Davos / Cramer / Stratechery-interview circuit
  [startuphub.ai 2026; Mad Money/CNBC; Stratechery 2026; arm.com/company/tech-unheard].
  He is *eminently* likeable — which is precisely the condition under which Munger
  says to distrust your own judgment.
- **The story is thrilling and simple** ("Arm taxes all of AI; AGI-CPU revenue
  will dwarf licensing in 5 years") — a narrative engineered for love, not for
  cash-flow scrutiny [startuphub.ai 2026, paraphrasing Haas].
- **The crowd is loving the story over the arithmetic.** Stock closed **$408.85
  (+15.7% on the day, ~doubled in two weeks)** at **~231x non-GAAP / ~481x GAAP**,
  **above** the average sell-side target (~$227–241) and above even most Street-high
  targets — i.e. the tape is *ahead of the people paid to be sober* (analysts), and
  targets are "following price, not leading it" [DOSSIER §3, §7]. The doubling came
  not on Arm's own numbers but on a **partner's** (Nvidia) launch, while Arm's own
  record Q4 *fell −7%* and management declined to raise guidance [DOSSIER §7].
  Sentiment is "ecosystem-narrative-driven, not ARM-guidance-driven" — love of the
  story, not the cash.
- **Independent voices are flagging exactly this.** "Valuation has disconnected
  from fundamentals"; "the market's rampant enthusiasm for AI"; rally "has run too
  far" [24/7 Wall St., 2026-05-11 and 2026-05-29; Simply Wall St., 2026].
- **The insiders who know it best are NOT in love at this price.** Net insider
  *selling*, no open-market buying; CEO and CFO part of ~$13.6M Q4 sales; Chief
  Architect holds **zero** direct shares [DOSSIER §6; 24/7 Wall St. 2026]. The
  people closest to the product are monetizing the market's affection, not adding
  to their own stakes.

## Reasoning

**Outward lens (customer love → pricing power & churn): partly true, eroding,
and the wrong *kind* of love.** Arm clearly has stickiness — but the model asks
specifically whether *affection* drives it. The evidence says the binding force
is **switching cost and best-available perf-per-watt, not love.** That distinction
matters because See's-candy/Coca-Cola "love" is *durable and self-reinforcing*
(customers actively want the brand), whereas tolerance-by-lock-in is *contingent*:
the moment a credible, royalty-free substitute (RISC-V) clears the toolchain bar,
or the moment the landlord becomes a competitor (AGI-CPU) and raises the rent
(royalty ladder, tightened TLAs), tolerant customers defect — and they are
*already pre-funding the defection* (Ventana, Google/Nvidia RISC-V). True love
doesn't behave that way. So on the outward read the model is **mildly negative**:
what looks like pricing power is real today but is being actively undermined by
the absence of genuine affection, and Arm's own strategy is accelerating the
erosion. This corroborates, from the psychology angle, the dossier's "wide but
softening" moat and the competition/RISC-V lanes.

**Inward lens (are WE being seduced?): strongly positive *as a warning* — this
is the model's loudest signal.** Every ingredient Munger names for Liking/Loving
distortion is present at maximum: a charismatic, near-universally-admired CEO; a
simple thrilling story; a price set by enthusiasm that has outrun the sober
professionals and the company's own guidance; and the tell-tale that the *adored
operators are selling into the affection.* Munger's instruction in this exact
situation is to invert: assume the love is doing the reasoning, strip it out, and
look only at the cash. When you do — owner-FCF ~zero-to-negative for three years,
clean ROIC only a thin spread over WACC, MoS ≈ −68% at midpoint [DOSSIER §2.2,
§2.4, §3.3] — the romance evaporates. The model's job here is to flag that the
*bull case is unusually love-laden* and therefore unusually likely to be
distorted. That is a meaningful, decision-relevant finding even though it is a
finding about us, not about Arm.

**Where this model is a stretch:** Liking/Loving is built for consumer
brand-affinity cases (See's, Coke, Apple, Harley). Arm is a B2B ingredient-IP
licensor; "customer love" maps imperfectly onto a royalty contract negotiated by
procurement and litigated in court. So the *outward* application is moderate-
relevance at best. The *inward* application (CEO/story seduction), however, is
highly relevant and is where I place my conviction. Net relevance is therefore
medium, weighted toward the bias-check.

**Not a fatal flaw.** This model is not fatal-capable (it is Psychology, not
incentives/inversion/accounting/margin-of-safety), and I have not found a fresh
*independent* path to permanent capital loss through this lens. The danger it
surfaces — overpaying because the story is lovable — is a *severe* contributor to
loss but it is the valuation/inversion lanes that own that verdict. I defer the
fatal call to them.

## Red flags

| Channel conflict from AGI-CPU vs the customers Arm needs goodwill from | high | Competing with your customers destroys affection precisely when it's the core asset [DOSSIER §1, §4.3]. |
| Adored-CEO + simple thrilling story driving a price ABOVE sober targets/guidance | high | The exact Liking/Loving setup Munger warns to distrust; tape doubled on a *partner's* news while Arm's own Q4 fell −7% [DOSSIER §3, §7]. |

| No transferable brand affinity (B2B ingredient, no end-user premium) | med | Limits durable pricing power; "love" is functional, not emotional (judgment, all sources). |
| Top customer litigated and largely won vs Arm | med | Affectionate customers don't take you to court and win; caps buyer-side goodwill [DOSSIER §8.4]. |

## What would flip this read (disconfirmers)

- **Customers stop building escape hatches:** Qualcomm/Google/Nvidia wind down or
  fail to ship RISC-V alternatives, and royalty-ladder hikes are accepted without
  litigation or diversification — evidence of genuine stickiness approaching love.
- **AGI-CPU coexists without channel revolt:** major customers publicly endorse and
  *expand* Arm commitments after the silicon pivot, showing the relationship
  survives Arm becoming a competitor.
- **The price re-anchors to cash, not story:** the stock falls back to within or
  below sober sell-side targets and trades on its own guidance rather than partner
  catalysts — removing the "we're in love" distortion and letting fundamentals lead.
- **Insiders buy in the open market** at these levels — a costly signal that the
  people closest to the product share the market's affection rather than selling it.
- **Evidence of true brand affinity emerges** (e.g., OEMs paying an Arm premium for
  trust/identity reasons, developer evangelism that resists a cheaper-equal
  substitute) — would upgrade the outward read from tolerance to love.

**Bottom line:** Customers depend on Arm but do not *love* it — the bond is
switching-cost and best-perf-per-watt tolerance, and Arm's own marquee customers
are actively pre-funding their exits (Qualcomm/Ventana, Google/Nvidia RISC-V) while
Arm corrodes goodwill by competing with them via the AGI-CPU. The far stronger
signal is inward: this is a near-textbook Liking/Loving trap *for the analyst* — an
adored CEO and a thrilling AI-toll-booth story have lifted the price above even the
sober professionals' targets and above the company's own guidance, with insiders
(including a Chief Architect who owns zero shares) selling into the affection.
Stripping out the love, as Munger demands, leaves cash flows that do not support
the romance. The model says: distrust the enthusiasm, and weight the disconfirmers.
