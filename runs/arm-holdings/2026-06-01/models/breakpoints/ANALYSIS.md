# Breakpoints / Thresholds — Arm Holdings (ARM)

**Discipline:** Physics  ·  **Intensity:** lens  ·  **As-of:** 2026-06-01

## What this model predicts

Most variables in a business move smoothly: a 5% change in inputs produces a ~5%
change in outputs. The Breakpoints lens hunts for the exceptions — the **phase
transitions**, the **tipping points**, the lines where a *small* move in an input
produces a *large, often discontinuous and sometimes irreversible* change in the
state of the system. In physics terms: where is the system near a critical point,
where susceptibility (dOutput/dInput) blows up? Classic financial breakpoints:

- A **covenant trip** or **debt-maturity wall** that flips solvent -> forced-seller.
- A **credit downgrade** across an investment-grade line that resets cost/availability of capital.
- A **churn / attach-rate threshold** where a flywheel reverses into a death spiral.
- A **regulatory line** (a merger block, a structural-separation order, a license ban) that re-rates the franchise overnight.
- A **valuation/reflexivity breakpoint** — where the *price itself* is the unstable state variable: a richly-priced momentum stock whose multiple, not its cash flows, is the thing one step from a discontinuous re-rating.

**If breakpoints are a benign feature here, we should observe:** a balance sheet far
from any covenant/maturity line; a valuation anchored to cash flows (so small
narrative shifts don't trigger large price moves); customer relationships and
royalty mechanics with smooth, hysteresis-free dynamics; and no single binary legal
or regulatory event that can flip the franchise's state. **If breakpoints are a
danger, we should observe** the opposite: identifiable lines, currently approached
or approachable, where small moves cascade.

## Evidence

**Supporting — breakpoints are MATERIAL and several are LIVE (cited):**

- **The valuation IS the unstable state variable (the dominant breakpoint).** ARM
  closed **$408.85 on 2026-06-01, +15.73% in one day, ~doubled in two weeks**, at
  **~231x non-GAAP / ~481x GAAP TTM P/E**, **~2.7x its 200-day MA ($152.18)**, with
  the price **ABOVE the average sell-side target (~$227-241)**. [Dossier section 3, 7]
  When price is set this far above any cash-flow anchor and above analysts' own
  targets, the supporting input is *narrative/multiple*, not earnings — and multiple
  is precisely the variable that moves discontinuously. This is a reflexive critical
  point: the move was triggered not by ARM's own results but by a **partner's
  (Nvidia's) Computex launch**, while ARM's *own* record Q4 fell **-7.25% on 5/7**.
  [Dossier section 3, 7] Susceptibility to non-fundamental news is, by observation, enormous.
- **The instability is quantified by the option market.** **Implied volatility 108.4%
  with IV Rank and IV Percentile both = 100** (top of their entire range), **beta
  3.78**, **PEG 5.29**. [Dossier section 7] IV at the 100th percentile is the market
  itself pricing a near-term phase transition — a fat-tailed, two-sided gap risk.
- **The math of the multiple breakpoint (own lens calc, judgment).** Even if ARM
  *hits* its bull **FY2031 >$9 non-GAAP EPS** plan, the 5-yr return from $408.85 is
  **-10.7%/yr at a 25x exit, -7.4% at 30x, -1.9% at 40x, +2.5% at 50x**. [Dossier section 3.2]
  Translation: the *price today already requires the market to STILL pay ~76x earnings
  in 2031.* The exit multiple is the knife-edge. A re-rate from ~163x fwd to a
  still-rich 40x — not a crash, just *normalization* — is a **~-75% price move**. The
  breakpoint here is not far in the tail; it is the base rate for what high-multiple
  growth names do when growth merely decelerates.
- **A concrete, near-term royalty-deceleration tripwire.** Royalty growth **slowed to
  +11% in Q4 FY2026 from +27% in Q3** — the first soft print of the year. [Dossier section 8.8]
  At 163x forward earnings, the difference between "royalties compounding 25%" and
  "royalties compounding 11%" is not linear in the stock; it is the narrative input
  that flips momentum. The dossier also flags **RPO (contracted backlog) FELL -7% YoY**
  even as ACV rose +22% — reduced forward visibility. [Dossier section 2.5]
- **SoftBank margin-loan / pledge = a genuine forced-seller breakpoint, geometry now
  partially knowable.** The 20-F discloses **$8.5B drawn, 769,029,000 shares (72.0% of
  the company) pledged**, foreclosure of which could **force a change of control with
  no offer to minority ADS holders**; exact LTV/trigger undisclosed. [Dossier section 6.1]
  Lens research fills the gap: the facility has been **expanded toward $20B ($8.5B
  drawn + ~$11.5B available, ~33-bank syndicate)** and SoftBank is actively seeking to
  draw more to fund **~$22.5B of OpenAI/Stargate commitments**. [Bloomberg 2025-10-10;
  Blockonomi/MEXC 2026; Dossier section 6.1] At the *current* $8.5B draw against ~923M
  beneficially-owned shares, a ~25% maintenance LTV implies a margin-call line in the
  **high-$30s/share** — i.e. **~-90% from $408.85, remote.** *But the breakpoint moves
  with the draw:* commentary models a maintenance breach near **~$80/share if the draw
  rises to ~$15B at 20% maintenance** (analyst estimate, not disclosed). [shanaka-
  anslemperera Substack; web search synthesis] So the forced-seller line is far today
  but **migrates upward, toward the price, exactly as SoftBank levers up** — a moving
  threshold, not a fixed one.
- **Parent-contagion threshold already demonstrated to be live.** A WSJ report (~2026-
  04-28) that **OpenAI missed revenue/user targets sent ARM ~-8% on the week**. [Dossier
  section 6.1] ARM's price is now coupled to **OpenAI/Stargate health** — a third-party
  milestone outside ARM's control acts as a switch on ARM's stock.
- **Regulatory structural-separation line (binary, irreversible if tripped).** The **US
  FTC opened a formal probe 2026-05-15** into whether ARM degrades/denies CPU licenses
  to Apple/Qualcomm/Nvidia while expanding its own AGI-CPU business; EU and Korea
  (KFTC, Seoul on-site Nov-2025) parallel actions. [Dossier section 8.5; Tom's Hardware
  2026-05-17; GuruFocus 2026-05] A structural-separation remedy or a forced
  must-license/non-discrimination order would re-rate the licensing-vs-silicon thesis
  in one step. This is a classic regulatory breakpoint: years of smooth operation,
  then a discontinuous order.
- **Qualcomm Case 2 (TLA/antitrust) -> TRIAL Q4 CY2026 — a binary catalyst.** ARM
  *largely LOST* Case 1 (Sept-30-2025 JMOL for Qualcomm; on appeal). [Dossier section 8.4]
  Case 2, with antitrust + Total-License-Agreement claims, is a near-term binary
  against a **9%-of-revenue customer**; an adverse TLA ruling could cap ARM's ability
  to tighten license terms industry-wide — a step-change in pricing power.
- **AGI-CPU is supply-capped (a hard, non-smooth constraint).** Demand "doubled to
  >$2B" but capacity (TSMC wafers/memory/packaging) secured for only ~$1B. [Dossier section 1,
  section 8.7] This is a literal capacity wall: above it, demand cannot convert to revenue.

**Disconfirming — where the dangerous breakpoints are ABSENT (cited):**

- **No covenant trip or debt-maturity wall at the operating company.** ARM has **net
  cash ~$3.1-3.2B, no public bonds, debt/equity 0.05, current ratio 6.0**; the ~$432M
  "debt" is almost entirely leases. [Dossier section 2.3] The leverage breakpoint sits at the
  *parent*, not at ARM. There is **nothing to downgrade** and no maturity to refinance.
- **No credit-downgrade breakpoint.** With no rated debt, the classic IG/HY line does
  not exist for ARM itself.
- **No customer churn cliff in the core franchise.** Smartphone app-processors **~99-
  100% Arm ISA**, automotive **~94%**, **>20M developers, >325B cumulative chips, high
  re-port switching costs**. [Dossier section 1, section 4.1] The ecosystem network effect has
  *hysteresis on ARM's side* — it resists rapid reversal. RISC-V is winning in MCU/IoT
  but is **NOT yet displacing ARM in smartphones or general servers**. [Dossier section 4.3]
  So the substitution breakpoint, while real long-term, is not near a tipping point in
  the revenue-heavy segments.
- **Accounting controls clean.** Disclosure controls **effective**; prior material
  weakness **remediated FY2025**; no restatement. [Dossier section 6] No accounting-cliff trigger.

## Reasoning

The Breakpoints lens is **highly relevant** here, but it reframes *which* breakpoint
matters. The instinctive places to look — covenants, downgrades, maturity walls — are
**empty**: ARM is a net-cash, unlevered, no-bond operating company (disconfirming
evidence, and genuinely reassuring on solvency). A naive screen would conclude "no
breakpoint risk." That would be the analytical error this lens exists to catch.

The real critical point is in the **price**, not the balance sheet. ARM at ~231x
non-GAAP earnings, ~2.7x its 200-DMA, *above* the average analyst target, with IV at
the 100th percentile, is a system sitting on a knife-edge where the load-bearing input
is the **exit multiple / narrative**, not cash flow. The defining property of a
breakpoint — that dOutput/dInput diverges — is satisfied: the stock moved ~+16% in a
day on a *partner's* product launch and -7% on its *own* record results. When a stock
ignores its own fundamentals and tracks ecosystem narrative, you are at a
reflexive critical point, and the move when the narrative input flips is large and
fast (the math says a *normalization* to 40x fwd — still a premium — is ~-75%). This is
the breakpoint that disqualifies the *price*, even though it does not disqualify the
*business*.

Layered on top are four **binary or migrating** thresholds, each of which can flip the
state in a step:
1. **SoftBank pledge** — a forced-seller mechanism that is *remote at today's draw*
   (line in the high-$30s) but **migrates toward the price as SoftBank levers up to fund
   OpenAI/Stargate**. A moving threshold is more dangerous than a fixed one because the
   counterparty's *own* incentive (raise cash) pushes the breakpoint toward the danger zone.
2. **FTC/EU/KFTC structural-separation** — years of smoothness, then a discontinuous order.
3. **Qualcomm Case-2 TLA trial (Q4 CY2026)** — a binary against a 9% customer that could
   cap industry-wide license-tightening power.
4. **Parent-contagion via OpenAI/Stargate** — already shown to transmit -8% on a single report.

These are *correlated*, which is the worst property for breakpoints: an AI-narrative
crack would simultaneously (a) compress ARM's multiple, (b) pressure OpenAI/Stargate
and SoftBank's NAV, (c) push SoftBank toward selling, and (d) hit the AGI-CPU demand
that justifies the premium. The dossier's own "existential-threat screen" reaches the
same conclusion: no single item is existential, but the **combination** defines the
left tail. [Dossier section 8] In breakpoints language: the individual lines are survivable,
but they are *coupled*, so the system can cascade through several at once.

**Check on our own reasoning (the lens turned inward).** Two biases to guard against.
First, *recency/anchoring on the +16% day*: it is tempting to call a top because the
move looks parabolic — but breakpoints are about *where the line is*, not about
predicting *when* it trips; a momentum stock can stay above its anchor for a long time
(the line can be approached and not crossed for quarters). I therefore frame this as
"the system is near a critical point with high susceptibility," **not** "it will crash
on date X." Second, *false precision on the margin-loan trigger*: the high-$30s/$80s
numbers are analyst back-of-envelope, not disclosed covenants; I weight them as
*directional* (the line exists and migrates with the draw) not *exact*. Honest
uncertainty: the single biggest unknown for this lens remains the **undisclosed
margin-loan LTV/trigger and current balance** — without it, the forced-seller
breakpoint can be *located approximately* but not *priced*. [Dossier section 9, gap #2]

Net: the dangerous breakpoints (solvency/covenant) are absent; the *relevant* ones
(multiple-reflexivity, a migrating forced-seller line, and two binary legal/regulatory
switches, all AI-narrative-correlated) are present and, in the case of valuation,
**already at the critical point.** Signal: negative.

## What would flip this read (disconfirmers)

- **Disclosure of the SoftBank margin-loan terms** showing a *low, fixed* LTV with a
  large buffer **and** a SoftBank commitment not to draw further against ARM — this
  would pin the forced-seller line far from price and stop it migrating, materially
  reducing that breakpoint.
- **A durable multiple reset toward fundamentals** — i.e. the stock trading back near
  the ~$227-241 analyst average and holding there on stable, growing royalties — would
  remove the reflexive valuation breakpoint (the price would no longer be the unstable
  variable). Conversely, *sustained* 25%+ royalty growth that the cash flows actually
  grow into would lengthen the runway and push the multiple-normalization pain further out.
- **FTC closing the probe with no action** and a **Qualcomm Case-2 settlement on terms
  that preserve ARM's licensing model** — removing the two binary legal/regulatory switches.
- **Decoupling from OpenAI/Stargate** (ARM trading independently of OpenAI headlines)
  would show the parent-contagion threshold is dormant rather than live.
- **Evidence the AGI-CPU and Armv9/CSS royalty ladder are compounding cash flow fast
  enough** that owner-FCF turns durably positive (it has been ~zero-to-negative for 3
  years once SBC is expensed, per Dossier section 2.2) — this would move the cash-flow
  anchor up under the price and shrink the multiple gap that makes the valuation a breakpoint.

**Bottom line:** The reassuring breakpoints (covenant trip, downgrade, maturity wall)
are absent — ARM is net-cash and unlevered, so the operating company itself is far from
any solvency line. But the *relevant* breakpoint is the **price**: at ~231x non-GAAP,
~2.7x its 200-DMA, above the average analyst target, with IV at the 100th percentile,
the load-bearing input is the multiple/narrative, and the stock has demonstrably moved
in large discontinuous steps on third-party news rather than its own results — a system
sitting on a reflexive critical point where mere *normalization* implies ~-75%.
Surrounding it are a **migrating** SoftBank forced-seller line (remote today but pushed
toward price as the parent levers up for OpenAI/Stargate) and two **binary** switches
(FTC structural-separation; Qualcomm Case-2 TLA trial), all correlated through the AI
narrative so they can cascade together. Negative signal, high relevance; the binding
constraint this lens surfaces is the valuation breakpoint, not a balance-sheet one.
