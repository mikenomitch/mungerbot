## DuPont decomposition (GAAP, average-equity basis)

| | FY2025 | FY2026 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Net margin | 19.8% | 18.4% |
| × Asset turnover | 0.47 | 0.50 |
| × Equity multiplier (leverage) | 1.40 | 1.30 |
| = **ROE** | 13.1% | 12.0% |

ROE is driven almost entirely by margin (asset-light, near-zero financial leverage). The decline in ROE FY25→FY26 reflects a lower net margin (rising R&D/SG&A and a return to a normal tax charge) and de-leveraging as the cash pile grows faster than it is deployed.

## Key ratios for downstream lanes
- **Net cash:** ~$3.17B (cash+STI $3.60B − total debt $0.43B). Effectively no financial leverage; ~$0.43B "debt" is almost entirely operating/finance leases — **no bond maturity wall.**
- **SBC-adjusted owner earnings:** FCF less equity-settled SBC was **negative in FY24 and FY25 and ~−$73M in FY26**. On a cash basis the company looks far stronger than on an SBC-burdened owner-earnings basis.
- **Capex inflection:** capex jumped from ~$92M (FY24) to **$545M (FY26)** — 11% of revenue — as Arm builds out compute for its own silicon (Arm AGI data-center CPU) and internal/AI infrastructure. This is a regime change for a historically capital-light IP licensor and compresses FCF.
