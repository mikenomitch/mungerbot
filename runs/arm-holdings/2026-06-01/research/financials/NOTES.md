## 1. Revenue & segment growth

Arm reports two revenue lines (no operating-segment P&L; one reportable segment):
- **Royalty revenue** — per-chip fees on shipments containing Arm IP (recurring, broad-based).
- **License & other revenue** — upfront/term license fees, ATA (Arm Total Access) subscriptions, software & services (lumpy, tied to deal timing).

| FY | Total rev | Royalty | License & other | Total YoY |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| FY2024 | $3,233M | ~$1,800M | ~$1,433M | +20.7% |
| FY2025 | $4,007M | $2,168M | $1,839M | +23.9% |
| FY2026 | $4,920M | $2,613M | $2,307M | +22.8% |

- **FY2026:** royalty +21% YoY to $2,613M; license & other +25% YoY to $2,307M (6-K). Q4 FY26 was the first ~$1.49B quarter; royalty Q4 +11%, license Q4 +29%.
- Growth is real and broad, but ~47% of FY26 revenue is the **lumpy license line** — high-value, timing-dependent deals (including backlog conversion) inflate single-year growth. FY2023 revenue actually *fell* 0.9% (cyclical handset weakness), a reminder royalties track the semiconductor unit cycle.
- Royalty growth is driven by adoption of newer architectures (Armv9 carries higher royalty rates) and content gains in data center / AI, automotive, and premium mobile (per shareholder letter).

## 2. Margins & their trend

- **Gross margin is exceptional and stable: 95–97.5%** (FY26 97.5%). Cost of sales is tiny (~$121M) — the hallmark of an IP licensor with negligible COGS. This is structurally near the ceiling.
- **GAAP operating margin is volatile: 23–25% (FY22–23) → 3.4% (FY24) → 20.7% (FY25) → 18.3% (FY26).** The FY24 collapse to 3.4% was driven by a one-time surge in **share-based compensation** when IPO-related RSUs vested (SBC jumped to $1,037M, ~32% of revenue). The post-IPO "normal" is high-teens to low-20s% GAAP operating margin, trending *down* as R&D and SG&A outgrow revenue.
- **R&D is the swing cost:** $995M (FY22) → $2,776M (FY26), now **56% of revenue.** R&D grew 34% in FY26 vs. revenue +23% — operating-margin compression is a deliberate reinvestment choice, not a demand problem.
- **GAAP net margin** ~18–20% in the normalized years (FY24 depressed to 9.5% by SBC).

## 3. ROIC vs WACC, ROE & DuPont

**WACC (third-party estimates):** ~10.5% (GuruFocus, 12/2025). Some screeners post wildly different WACCs (one source 24%+) due to high beta and methodology; ~9–11% is the defensible range for a net-cash, low-beta-debt, high-equity-cost name. **Data gap:** no single authoritative WACC; treat as ~10%.

**ROIC — wide dispersion across sources; methodology matters enormously** because of the ~$3.6B cash pile and ~$1.05B annual SBC:
- Mixed third-party ROIC: ~11% (stockanalysis ratios), ~14.3–14.8% (stockanalysis stats / GuruFocus 12/2025), and FY25 as high as ~17%.
- **My clean calc (FY2026):** NOPAT = GAAP operating income $900M × (1 − 22% tax) = **$702M**.
  - Invested capital **excluding** excess cash = equity $8,286M + debt $432M − cash&investments $3,601M = $5,117M → **ROIC ≈ 13.7%.**
  - Including cash in IC → ~8.1%.
  - On **tangible** IC (also stripping $1,623M goodwill + $230M intangibles) → ~21.5%.
- **Conclusion:** On an excess-cash-adjusted basis ROIC is roughly **13–15% vs. a ~10% WACC — a modest positive spread**, not a wide-moat-style 30%+. Crucially, if SBC is treated as a real cash-equivalent cost (it dilutes owners ~$1B/yr), economic ROIC is materially lower. The spread is positive but thin relative to what a ~$300B+ market cap implies.

**ROE & DuPont (GAAP, average-equity basis):**
| | FY2025 | FY2026 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Net margin | 19.8% | 18.4% |
| × Asset turnover | 0.47 | 0.50 |
| × Equity multiplier | 1.40 | 1.30 |
| = ROE | **13.1%** | **12.0%** |
- ROE is **all margin** — asset-light, essentially zero financial leverage (equity multiplier ~1.3 and falling as cash builds). ROE *declined* FY25→FY26 because margin fell and the firm de-levered (cash growing faster than deployed). A 12% ROE is unremarkable for a "compounder" and reflects a cash-heavy, under-levered balance sheet plus SBC-driven margin pressure.

## 4. Free cash flow & conversion

| FY | OCF | Capex | FCF | FCF margin | FCF/NI |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| FY2022 | 458 | (34) | 424 | 15.7% | 77% |
| FY2023 | 739 | (64) | 675 | 25.2% | 129% |
| FY2024 | 1,090 | (92) | 998 | 30.9% | 326% |
| FY2025 | 397 | (219) | 178 | 4.4% | 22% |
| FY2026 | 1,524 | (545) | 979 | 19.9% | 108% |

- **OCF and FCF are highly volatile** year to year — driven by (a) working-capital swings on lumpy licensing/collections and (b) the FY25 anomaly. FY2025 OCF cratered to $397M (FCF $178M) largely on a large negative working-capital swing (AR build, deferred-tax/accrued-comp movements); FY2026 OCF rebounded to $1,524M. Do **not** annualize a single year.
- **FCF conversion looks "fine" on a GAAP-NI basis (108% FY26)** but this is flattered because GAAP NI is *itself* suppressed by SBC while OCF *adds SBC back*. The honest owner-earnings view:
  - **FCF less equity-settled SBC** = $(73)M (FY26), $(642)M (FY25), $(39)M (FY24). I.e., once you charge stock comp as the real cost of labor it is, Arm has produced **roughly zero-to-negative owner free cash flow in each of the last three fiscal years.** This is the single most important earnings-quality fact in this lane.
- **Capex regime change** (see §6) is now a structural drag on FCF.

## 5. Balance sheet, net debt, maturity wall, liquidity

- **Net cash ≈ $3.17B** (FY26): cash $2,751M + ST investments $850M = $3,601M, less total debt $432M.
- **No meaningful financial debt / no maturity wall.** Total "debt" of ~$432M is almost entirely **operating and finance lease liabilities** (debt/equity 0.05). There are no public bonds and no near-term refinancing risk. Covenant risk is immaterial.
- **Liquidity is fortress-like:** current ratio 6.0x, quick ratio ~4.7x, working capital $5,196M.
- **Equity $8,286M**, of which goodwill $1,623M + intangibles $230M (~22% of equity is intangible); tangible book ~$6.4B. Retained earnings $4,447M (positive and growing — no accumulated deficit).
- **Pension/lease:** non-current accrued comp $32M; lease obligations embedded in the ~$432M debt. No defined-benefit pension red flag surfaced. **Data gap:** exact gross operating-lease commitment schedule (in the 20-F notes, not re-pulled here).

## 6. Working capital & capex intensity

- **DSO is high: ~96 days (FY26), ~101 days (FY25)** — elevated for a software-like licensor, partly structural (term-license billings) and partly **related-party:** accounts receivable include **$270M due from related parties (SoftBank group) at 3/31/26 ($279M at 3/31/25).** AR rose to $1,300M (+17% YoY) — a working-capital use; not alarming but a line the earnings-quality lane should watch for revenue-pull-forward / collection risk given SoftBank concentration.
- **Capex intensity inflected hard:** 1.3% of revenue (FY22) → 2.8% (FY24) → 5.5% (FY25) → **11.1% (FY26)**; absolute capex $34M → $545M. P&E on the balance sheet doubled to $772M. This reflects Arm's move into building its **own silicon** (the "Arm AGI" data-center CPU launched FY26) and AI/compute infrastructure — a strategic shift away from the pure capital-light licensing model. Maintenance vs. growth split is not disclosed; treat ~$50–100M as maintenance and the balance as growth. **This is the most important *change* in the financial profile.**

## 7. Share count, dilution, buybacks, dividends

- **Diluted weighted-avg shares rose every year: 1,025M (FY22) → 1,068M (FY26).** Issued shares 1,064M at 3/31/26 (up from 1,057M at 3/31/25).
- **Dilution is from SBC** (RSUs) and the ESPP ($74M proceeds FY26, $39M Q4). There is **no buyback program** and **no dividend** (Arm states it intends to retain all earnings; FAQ + prospectus). Capital is returned to *employees* via equity, not to *shareholders*.
- Net effect: an owner's per-share claim is being **diluted ~0.5–1%/yr** with no offsetting repurchase — compounding the SBC earnings-quality issue.

## 8. Accounting red flags / earnings quality

1. **SBC is enormous and treated as "non-GAAP" away:** $1,052M equity-settled SBC (FY26) ≈ **21% of revenue**; plus ~$150M employer payroll taxes on SBC. The **non-GAAP net income ($1,889M) is ~2.1× GAAP net income ($904M)**, and the bridge is dominated by adding back SBC ($1,052M) + employer SBC taxes ($150M) and several **one-time gains** (FY26 non-GAAP also benefits from +$131M reversed in "other," a $127M divestiture, equity-investment gains $205M, loan settlement $56M — non-recurring). **The market/headlines anchor on non-GAAP EPS ($1.77) that excludes a genuine, recurring ~$1B cost.** This is the central earnings-quality caveat.
2. **FCF-less-SBC ≈ zero/negative for 3 straight years** (see §4) — the strongest single quality flag.
3. **Lumpy license revenue (47% of total)** inflates growth optics in strong-backlog years; revenue-recognition timing on multi-year term licenses + ATA subscriptions warrants scrutiny (the *filings* lane should confirm rev-rec policy and any changes). RPO actually **fell 7% YoY to $2,071M** while ACV rose 22% to $1,660M — a divergence that *reduces* forward revenue visibility vs. a year ago and is worth flagging.
4. **Related-party concentration:** $270M AR from SoftBank group; numerous related-party arrangements (Arm China is a notable historical complication). High DSO partly reflects this. The *filings/legal* lanes should detail related-party transactions and the Arm China relationship.
5. **Rising capitalization:** intangible-asset purchases ($30M) and the capex surge mean more cost is on the balance sheet vs. expensed; D&A rising ($249M) will follow. Not aggressive yet, but the trend bears watching as the silicon strategy scales.
6. **Tax volatility:** FY25 booked a tax *benefit* (+$72M); FY26 a normal charge ($253M, ~22%). Net income comparisons across years are distorted by tax-line swings and large deferred-tax movements (deferred tax −$214M one quarter). Use pre-tax/operating trends, not reported net income, for trend analysis.

No restatement, going-concern, or auditor-change red flag surfaced in this lane (confirm via *filings* lane — auditor, internal-control attestation, any material weakness).
