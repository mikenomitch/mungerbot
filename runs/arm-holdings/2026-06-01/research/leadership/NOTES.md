## 0. The headline governance fact: SoftBank control

Arm is a **SoftBank-controlled company**. SoftBank Group beneficially owns **~86.4%** of issued and outstanding share capital and a majority of voting power (stated "as of May 21, 2026" in the FY2026 20-F). Consequences:

- Arm is a **"controlled company" under Nasdaq rules** and **relies on exemptions** from certain governance requirements that protect minority holders (e.g., majority-independent board / fully independent nominating & comp committees).
- A **Shareholder Governance Agreement** gives SoftBank the right to **designate a number of board candidates** scaled to its ownership. "Other holders of ordinary shares and ADSs will have limited ability to influence matters requiring shareholder approval or the composition of the Board."
- Practical effect: **minority shareholders are along for the ride.** Any AGM vote (including the CEO pay package below) is effectively decided by SoftBank. The public float is only ~13–14%, which also amplifies share-price volatility (see news/supply-demand lanes).
- **Forced-seller tail risk:** SoftBank took an **$8.5B margin loan against its Arm stock (capacity for ~$11.5B more)** to help fund commitments including ~$22.5B to OpenAI. A sharp Arm drawdown could pressure SoftBank; this links SoftBank's separate balance-sheet stress to Arm's stock.
  - Sources: Arm 20-F FY2026 (controlled-company / governance-agreement language, via SEC WebSearch); Motley Fool 2026-01-07 (margin loan, "~87%"); TradingKey (SoftBank 87% / ~$40B cumulative cost). [20-F FY2026](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001973239/000197323926000097/arm-20260331.htm) · [Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/01/07/why-arm-holdings-plunged-nearly-20-in-december/)

## 4. Capital allocation — "what was done with a dollar of retained earnings?"

Arm's capital-allocation history splits into **pre-IPO (SoftBank era)** and **post-IPO (public)**:

**Reinvestment (the dominant use).** Arm is fundamentally an **R&D-reinvestment** story: it plows cash into engineering (CPU/GPU IP, Compute Subsystems, now its own data-center "AGI" CPU effort) rather than returning capital. FY2026 revenue **$4.92B (+23%)**: royalty $2.613B (+21%), licensing/other $2.307B (+25%). No dividend.
- Source: [Arm Q4/FYE26 results](https://newsroom.arm.com/news/arm-q4-fye26-results); SeekingAlpha/TradingView FY26 reviews.

**Dividends:** **None.** Arm pays no dividend; capital is retained for growth. (Source: AMD-vs-Arm comparison; consistent across coverage.)

**Buybacks:** **Token.** ~**$119M repurchased in a single quarter (Q3 FY26)**, explicitly **not enough to offset** new shares issued for stock-based comp. Diluted share count still **rose** modestly FY25→FY26 (~1,063M → ~1,068M). So buybacks are an SBC-mop, not real capital return.
- Sources: Motley Fool / Q3 coverage ($119M buyback, $285M SBC in the quarter ⇒ SBC running ~$1B+/yr and exceeding buybacks). [Form S-8 FY26](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001973239/000197323926000066/armholdings-formsx8refresh.htm) registered 13.8M more shares for incentive plans.

**M&A track record (mixed; mostly under SoftBank).** Arm has done ~26 acquisitions historically but is **not** an aggressive acquirer now (avg ~0.2/yr over 2020–2025):
- IoT era (SoftBank-directed): **Treasure Data (~$600M, 2018)**, **Apical (~$350M, 2016)**, Stream Technologies, etc. — built an "IoT Services Group" that was later **separated from Arm's P&L pre-IPO** to clean up the chip-IP story (the **2020 plan to transfer IoT to SoftBank was announced, then reversed**, then the businesses were ultimately carved out/segregated to present Arm as a pure IP company for the IPO). Net: substantial sums spent on an IoT strategy that was **walked back** — a knock on prior (SoftBank-era) capital allocation, though predating Haas-as-CEO.
- Recent: **DreamBig Semiconductor-type tuck-in ("DreamBigSemi", ~$265M, 2025)**; equity stakes for ecosystem (e.g., **Kigen**) classified as long-term strategic.
- Sources: Wikipedia (Arm; Treasure Data); Tracxn acquisitions list; TechCrunch (Treasure Data 2018); RCR Wireless / The Register / TechCrunch (2020 IoT spin-off and reversal). [Treasure Data/TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2018/08/02/arm-acquires-data-management-service-treasure-data-to-bolster-its-iot-platform/) · [IoT to SoftBank then reversed](https://www.theregister.com/2020/08/26/arm_iot_platforms_softbank/)

**Capital-allocation read (judgment):**
- The current capital model is **"retain everything, reinvest in R&D, no real return of capital,"** which is defensible for a high-ROIC IP licensor in a growth phase — *if* reinvestment earns its keep (ROIC question is for the financials/valuation lanes). 
- **Dilution leakage** via SBC that buybacks don't fully offset is a per-share-value watch item.
- The **balance sheet is ~net cash** (negligible debt, ~$2B+ cash + investments per snapshot) — i.e., Arm is *not* leveraging up or doing financial engineering itself; the leverage sits at the **SoftBank parent** (margin loan), not at Arm.
- **Haas-era** record is short and dominated by the IPO and reinvestment; the messier capital decisions (Arm China sale, IoT over-build/reversal, the $40B SoftBank cost basis) belong to the **SoftBank era** and bear on *controller* rationality more than on Haas.

## 5. Arm China — the standing related-party / control flag

A material, still-relevant governance scar:
- SoftBank **sold 51% of Arm China to a Chinese consortium for ~$775.2M (2018)**; Arm/SoftBank retained ~**47–49%**.
- From **2020–2022**, ousted Arm China CEO/Chair **Allen Wu** (who controlled a ~**16% stake**) **refused to leave**, physically retained the **company chop/seal and registration documents**, and ran the JV independently. Whistleblowers alleged **self-dealing**: steering Arm China customers toward his personal VC fund **Alphatecture** (set up July 2019) in exchange for discounts.
- Arm **regained board control in 2022** (Wu removed; co-CEOs Liu Renchen & Eric Chen appointed) — a prerequisite for the IPO.
- **Why it still matters:** Arm China is **deconsolidated** yet is a **major channel/customer** for Arm IP in the largest semiconductor market; it is a related party with a history of governance failure and sits inside **US–China tech-tension** crosshairs. Royalty collection, IP control, and disclosure quality there are ongoing risk items (hand to legal-regulatory and competition lanes).
- Sources: SCMP, The Register, IT Pro, Asianometry, Tom's Hardware (2020–2022). [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3175833/battle-arm-china-heats-softbank-launches-renewed-attempt-take-control) · [Arm regains control](https://www.mobileworldlive.com/softbank/arm-regains-control-of-china-joint-venture/)

## 7. Founder involvement
- Arm's technical roots trace to **Acorn Computers / the original ARM (Advanced RISC Machines) founders (1990)**; none of the 1990-era founders are in management or on the board today. There is **no founder-operator** in the Munger "owner-CEO with skin in the game" sense.
- The closest analog to a "founder" today is **Masayoshi Son** as the controlling owner-chairman (founder of *SoftBank*, not of Arm). His imprint — appetite for scale, leverage at the parent, the OpenAI/AI mega-bets — is the dominant strategic influence over Arm's destiny.

## 8. Net governance scorecard (evidence, not verdict)

**Positives**
- Capable, credentialed, well-regarded CEO (Haas) and CFO (Child); strong IPO execution; revenue roughly doubled under Haas.
- Pay is heavily **at-risk / performance-linked**; the proposed mega-grant only pays on enormous value creation (real alignment *if* not later re-priced).
- **Strong employee sentiment** (Glassdoor 4.5, 93% CEO approval).
- **Net-cash balance sheet**; no financial-engineering leverage *at Arm*.
- Some independent directors with real CFO/operator pedigree (Dykstra, Schooler, Jacobs).

**Negatives / watch items**
- **Severe minority-governance weakness:** SoftBank ~86.4% control, controlled-company **exemptions**, board **not majority-independent**, Shareholder Governance Agreement → minority votes (incl. say-on-pay) are effectively non-binding. Thin ~13% float.
- **CEO conflict / divided attention:** Haas is **also CEO of SoftBank Group International**, overseeing the parent's chip/AI portfolio — related-party and time-allocation risk.
- **Pay magnitude:** ~$60M FY26 single figure (~4x size-peer median) and a proposed **~$800M–$1bn+** grant whose "approval" SoftBank controls.
- **Insiders are net sellers; no open-market buying.** Direct stakes modest vs comp; Chief Architect holds **0** direct shares.
- **Dilution leakage:** SBC (~$1B+/yr) exceeds token buybacks (~$119M/qtr); share count drifting up.
- **Arm China** related-party/control scar and ongoing channel risk in the key China market.
- **Parent contagion:** SoftBank's **$8.5B+ margin loan against Arm stock** (OpenAI funding) = forced-seller tail risk transmitted from controller to the float.
- **Capital-allocation history is short under Haas** and the longer (SoftBank-era) record includes a **walked-back IoT build-out** and the Arm China sale.
