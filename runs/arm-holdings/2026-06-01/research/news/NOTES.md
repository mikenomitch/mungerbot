## 1. Executive digest (signal, not noise)

Arm is in the middle of the most consequential strategic shift in its history, and the last 18
months are dominated by four intertwined storylines:

1. **From pure-IP licensor to chip vendor.** On **2026-03-24** Arm launched the **Arm AGI CPU**,
   its first in-house, Arm-designed *production silicon* in ~35 years (a 136-core Neoverse-V3,
   TSMC 3nm data-center CPU), with **Meta** as co-developer and lead customer. This breaks Arm's
   long-standing "Switzerland of chips" neutrality and puts it in direct competition with the
   licensees that pay its royalties. Strong stated demand but a hard supply ceiling (below).

2. **Royalty + licensing both compounding >20% on AI/data-center pull.** FY2026 (FYE 2026-03-31)
   was the **third straight year of >20% revenue growth** post-IPO: **$4.92B** revenue, royalty
   **$2.61B** (+21%), licensing **$2.31B** (+25%), record non-GAAP EPS **$1.77**. Data-center
   royalties **more than doubled YoY for two consecutive quarters**. Every FY2026 quarter beat and
   guided up; the stock is up sharply in 2026.

3. **Arm *lost* its marquee litigation with Qualcomm**, and the dispute has metastasized into a
   **multi-jurisdiction antitrust problem for Arm**. Qualcomm won the Dec-2024 jury trial and the
   Sept/Oct-2025 final judgment; Qualcomm then filed antitrust complaints (FTC / EU / Korea) in
   Mar-2025, and the **U.S. FTC opened a formal antitrust probe of Arm on 2026-05-15** — two weeks
   before this as-of date.

4. **SoftBank's gravitational pull intensifies.** SoftBank (Arm's ~87%-90% owner) co-founded the
   **$500B Stargate** AI-infra JV (Jan-2025, Arm a named tech partner) and **acquired Ampere
   Computing for $6.5B** (announced Mar-2025, closed 2025-11-25), buying out — among others — Arm's
   own 8.08% Ampere stake. Arm's fortunes are now tightly coupled to AI-data-center capex and to
   SoftBank's AI build-out, which makes AI-demand scares (e.g., the OpenAI revenue-miss reports)
   move the stock hard.

**Two big risk threads to carry downstream:** (a) the **gross-margin and R&D-spend dilution** from
becoming a merchant-silicon vendor (Morgan Stanley downgrade, Apr-2026), and (b) the **antitrust /
customer-conflict overhang** now that Arm both licenses to and competes with Apple/Qualcomm/Nvidia.

## 3. The strategic pivot: Arm AGI CPU (in-house silicon) — the dominant story

**Event:** Arm unveiled the **Arm AGI CPU** on **2026-03-24** at an Arm event in San Francisco —
its **first Arm-designed, Arm-sold production chip in ~35 years**. CEO **Rene Haas**:
*"Today marks the next phase of the Arm compute platform and a defining moment for our company."*
(Arm Newsroom, https://newsroom.arm.com/news/arm-agi-cpu-launch, 2026-03-24)

**Specs / claims (Arm-stated):**
- Up to **136 Arm Neoverse V3 cores** per CPU; **TSMC 3nm**; **300W TDP**.
- **">2x performance per rack vs x86"**; up to **8,160 cores/rack** air-cooled (1U) or **45,000+**
  liquid-cooled; 6 GB/s bandwidth/core at sub-100ns latency.
- Pitched as up to **"$10B CAPEX savings per gigawatt"** of AI data-center capacity.
- **Manufactured by TSMC** (3nm, in Taiwan). Arm is fabless here — it designs and sells, TSMC fabs.

**Customers / ecosystem (Arm-named):**
- **Lead partner / co-developer: Meta** (building multi-GW AI data centers; FY2026 capex guided up
  to ~$135B per secondary coverage — confirm with Meta filings, out of this lane's scope).
- **Named launch customers/partners:** OpenAI, Cloudflare, Cerebras, F5, Positron, Rebellions,
  SAP, SK Telecom.
- **OEM/ODM:** ASRock Rack, Lenovo, Quanta, Supermicro.
- **Broader ecosystem (50+):** AWS, Broadcom, Google, Marvell, Micron, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Samsung,
  SK hynix, TSMC.

**Timeline:** Early systems available at launch; **broader availability H2 2026**; **first AGI-CPU
production *revenue* expected Q4 FY2027** (i.e., Jan–Mar 2027 quarter); volume ramp into 2027–2028;
"material revenue in 2028" per some analyst framing.

**Demand vs. supply — the load-bearing nuance (and a resolved figure conflict):**
- On the **2026-05-06** Q4 call, **Haas said AGI-CPU customer demand across FY2027–FY2028 "has
  already doubled from $1 billion to more than $2 billion."** Management is holding a **$1B AGI-CPU
  *revenue* target** with first revenue in Q4 FY2027. (BigGo transcript recap, quoting Haas.)
- **The supply ceiling is the catch:** Arm has secured manufacturing capacity (wafers / memory /
  packaging / test at TSMC) for only roughly the **first ~$1B**; teams are "working around the
  clock" to source capacity for the *second* billion. **Supply, not demand, is the constraint.**
- **⚠ Figure conflict / DATA GAP:** Several outlets (fxleaders, tikr) reported Haas saying demand
  "doubled to **over $20 billion** in six weeks." A transcript-based recap (BigGo) instead quotes
  **"$1B → >$2B."** I judge the **~$2B figure to be the correct demand pipeline** (it's a direct
  quote and is internally consistent with the $1B revenue target and the ~$1B secured capacity);
  the "$20B" appears to be a reporting error or a conflation with a larger TAM/total-demand number.
  **The filings lane should confirm against the primary shareholder letter / official transcript.**

**Why it matters (themes for the model agents — stated as facts, not opinions):**
- **Margin structure changes.** As a >95%-gross-margin IP licensor, selling merchant silicon drags
  blended gross margin toward **40–50%** on the silicon revenue (Morgan Stanley est.). This is a
  genuine business-model mix shift, not just a one-off.
- **R&D / opex step-up.** Arm built the chip over ~18 months in Austin, TX, reportedly investing
  ~$71M and expanding the silicon team past 1,000 engineers; opex guidance rose (Q1 FY27 non-GAAP
  opex ~$760M vs ~$655M a year earlier).
- **Channel conflict / neutrality break.** Arm now competes with the customers that pay its
  royalties — central to the antitrust thread in §5.

## 4. Qualcomm litigation — Arm LOST (decided in the window)

A multi-year, high-stakes dispute over whether Qualcomm's **Nuvia**-derived **Oryon** CPU cores were
covered by Qualcomm's own Arm Architecture License Agreement (ALA). Outcome: **a clean,
decisive loss for Arm.**

- **2024-10-23 — Escalation:** Arm issued Qualcomm a **60-day cancellation notice** for its
  architectural license, threatening to force Qualcomm to halt sales of Arm-based products
  (Snapdragon PC/phone chips) if unresolved. Read as a pressure tactic ahead of trial. (Bloomberg /
  Reuters / TechCrunch, 2024-10-23.)
- **Dec 2024 — Jury trial (Delaware):** Jury found **unanimously that Qualcomm did NOT breach** the
  Nuvia ALA and that the Oryon cores were properly licensed under Qualcomm's ALA. The jury
  **deadlocked (mistrial) on whether *Nuvia* breached** its agreement. Net: a Qualcomm win; Arm did
  not get the verdict it sought. (Engadget, Tom's Hardware, DCD.)
- **Sept/Oct 2025 — Final judgment:** The Delaware U.S. District Court ruled **neither Qualcomm nor
  Nuvia breached** Arm's ALA, **dismissed Arm's remaining claim, and denied Arm's request for a new
  trial.** Qualcomm called it a **"complete victory."** (Qualcomm IR press release, 2025-09/10,
  https://www.qualcomm.com/news/releases/2025/09/qualcomm-achieves-complete-victory-over-arm-in-litigation-challe;
  The Register, 2025-10-01; Paul Weiss client note; RCR Wireless.)

**Significance:** Beyond the (limited) direct financial impact, the loss (a) undercut Arm's leverage
over architecture licensees, (b) emboldened Qualcomm, and (c) fed directly into the antitrust
counter-offensive in §5. The license-cancellation threat against Qualcomm appears to have lapsed
given the rulings (confirm current ALA status with filings/legal lane).

## 5. Antitrust / regulatory overhang on Arm (escalating; fresh as of as-of date)

This is the **single newest material development** and is two weeks old at the as-of date.

- **March 2025 — Qualcomm's global antitrust campaign:** Qualcomm filed simultaneous competition
  complaints with the **U.S. FTC, the European Commission, and South Korea's KFTC**, alleging Arm
  abuses its dominant licensing position while selling its own processors — specifically by
  restricting/withholding access to CSS reference designs and degrading license quality to benefit
  Arm's own chip business. (Tom's Hardware / Slashdot / Yahoo Finance, 2025-03-27.)
- **2025-11 — Korea on-site inspection:** KFTC conducted an **on-site inspection of Arm's Seoul
  office** over the alleged unfair licensing practices. (Korea Times 2025-11-19; Digitimes 2025-11-20.)
- **Early 2026 — European Commission** opened its own investigation (per techtimes summary; confirm
  exact EC docket/date with the legal-regulatory lane).
- **2026-05-15 — U.S. FTC opens FORMAL antitrust probe of Arm** (first reported by Bloomberg
  2026-05-15). The FTC is examining whether Arm intends to **refuse CPU-design licenses to rival
  chip designers or degrade those licenses' quality** while expanding its own (AGI-CPU) chip
  business — i.e., whether the in-house-silicon move lets Arm squeeze the very licensees
  (**Apple, Qualcomm, Nvidia** named) it now competes with. Status: evidence-gathering / document-
  preservation demand issued. **Arm declined to comment**; it had earlier called Qualcomm's
  allegations **"baseless."** (TechTimes 2026-05-17,
  https://www.techtimes.com/articles/316752/20260517/...; Bloomberg via bloomingbit.)

**Tail-risk note for the legal-regulatory lane:** an adverse antitrust remedy could constrain Arm's
freedom to favor its own silicon, or force structural separation of licensing vs. chip business —
directly attacking the AGI-CPU thesis. Not existential on current facts, but a real, live overhang.
Sibling lane `legal-regulatory` owns the deeper write-up; flagged here because it is also the
freshest *catalyst*.

## 7. Competitive / product-cycle news threads (signal)

- **RISC-V threat intensifying.** Arm's 20-F risk factors and multiple analysts flag open-source
  RISC-V as a structural threat, *especially in China* (state-backed; "first fully self-developed
  high-performance RISC-V server chip" cited). Arm's China growth is reportedly soft partly due to
  RISC-V substitution. (Arm 20-F FY2025 risk factors; Design-Reuse; Seeking Alpha
  "Premium Valuation At Risk With China's RISC-V Gambit", 2025.)
- **2025-12 — Qualcomm acquires Ventana Micro (RISC-V CPU designer)** — widely read as a deliberate
  hedge against Arm licensing risk, hard on the heels of Qualcomm's courtroom win. A customer
  building an Arm off-ramp. (Per techtimes/Seeking Alpha summaries; confirm deal terms with
  competition lane.)
- **Armv9 + CSS mix-up = higher royalty/chip.** Recurring management theme all year: rising Armv9
  adoption and Compute Subsystems (CSS) shipments lift royalty *per chip*, a key royalty-growth
  driver independent of unit volume. (Arm Newsroom quarterly releases.)
- **Smartphone + automotive + edge/physical AI** cited as broad-based royalty contributors;
  data-center is the standout (royalty >2x YoY for two straight quarters).
